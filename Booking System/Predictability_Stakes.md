## PREDICTABILITY INDEX

### How It's Calculated

**Formula:** Predictability Index = (Pattern Recognition Score Ã— Booking Repetition) / Surprise Factor

**Pattern Recognition Score:**

- Counts similar booking decisions
- Threshold: 3-5 repetitions = pattern recognized
- Examples: Champion always retains on TV, babyface always kicks out at 2.9, heel always cheats with distraction

**Booking Repetition:**

- How often same pattern repeats
- Frequency multiplier (weekly = worse than monthly)

**Surprise Factor:**

- Recent unexpected outcomes reduce index
- Clean finishes when screwy expected
- Upsets when favorite was obvious

## COMMON PREDICTABLE PATTERNS

### Pattern Library (Crowds Learn These)

| Pattern | Description | Effect After Recognition |
|---------|-------------|-------------------------|
| **Champion Always Retains on TV** | Title matches on weekly TV never result in title changes | TV title matches get weak reactions, crowd doesn't believe threat |
| **Contract Signing = Brawl** | Every contract signing segment ends in violence | Crowd chants for fight before it happens, surprise is dead |
| **Babyface Kickout at 2.9** | Hero always kicks out of finisher at last moment | Near-falls lose power, crowd knows outcome |
| **Heel Distraction Finish** | Heel always wins via manager/partner distraction | Crowd anticipates, heat diminishes |

| **PPV = Screwy Finish** | Big matches always end with interference/DQ | Crowd expects no clean finishes, disappointed |

**Threshold:** Pattern becomes predictable after **3-5 occurrences**

## STAKES WEIGHTING SYSTEM

### Stakes Hierarchy

| Stakes Type | Crowd Investment Multiplier | Risk | Notes |
|-------------|---------------------------|------|-------|
| **No Stakes** | 0.5x | None | Forgettable, filler match |
| **Personal Pride** | 1.0x | Low | Baseline, "just want to win" |
| **Grudge Match** | 1.3x | Medium | Personal animosity, recent feud |
| **Title Match** | 1.8x | High | Championship on the line |
| **Number One Contender** | 1.5x | Medium-High | Future title shot at stake |
| **Career vs. Career** | 3.0x | Maximum | Loser retires, emotional |
| **Loser Leaves Town** | 2.5x | Very High | Rare, high-impact |
| **Mask vs. Mask** (Lucha) | 3.5x | Sacred | Cultural maximum stakes |
| **Hair vs. Hair** | 2.0x | High | Humiliation, traditional |
| **Bloodline/Legacy** | 2.2x | High | Family honor, generational |

### Stakes Matching

**Overstaked:**

- Stakes too high for feud intensity
- Example: Career match after 1-month program
- **Effect:** Feels forced, crowd doesn't buy it

**Understaked:**

- Stakes too low for feud emotion
- Example: No-DQ match for minor grudge gets normal singles match
- **Effect:** Feels anticlimactic, missed opportunity

**Optimal:**

- Stakes match emotional buildup
- Example: 6-month blood feud â†’ cage match or retirement match
- **Effect:** Satisfying payoff, crowd fully invested

## UNDERDOG MECHANICS

### Underdog Pop Bonus

**When:** Lower-ranked worker defeats heavily-favored opponent

**Formula:** Underdog Pop = (Opponent Pop - Underdog Pop) Ã— Surprise Factor Ã— Stakes Multiplier

**Example:**

- Opponent Pop: 85 (top star)
- Underdog Pop: 45 (midcarder)
- Surprise Factor: High (1.5x)
- Stakes: Title match (1.8x)
- **Underdog Bonus:** (85-45) Ã— 1.5 Ã— 1.8 = **+108 momentum spike**

**Requirements:**

- Must be believable struggle (no squash)
- Clean finish preferred (dirty win reduces bonus)
- Story must support upset (can't be random)

**Risk of Overuse:**

- Too many upsets = nothing feels special
- Top stars lose credibility
- Threshold: 1-2 major upsets per year maximum

## FAN SPECULATION BONUS

### Genuine Uncertainty

**When both workers could realistically win:**

- Neither is obviously stronger
- Story supports either outcome
- Betting odds are close
- Smart fans genuinely debate

**Effect:**

- +10 to +20 match rating bonus
- Higher crowd investment
- Post-match reaction amplified
- Long-term memory retention improved

**"Dream Match" Multiplier:**

- Two top stars, both protected
- First-time match or rare match-up
- High stakes
- **Speculation Bonus:** Ã—2.0 investment

**Examples:**

- Rock vs. Hogan (WrestleMania X8)
- Omega vs. Okada I

## BOOKING CORNER PENALTIES

### When Predictability Kills Quality

**Scenario:** Great in-ring match, but outcome was obvious

**Penalty:** -5 to -10 match rating due to predictability

**Example:**

- Match quality: 4.5 stars (objectively great)
- But: Everyone knew champion would retain on TV (pattern)
- **Final Rating:** 3.5-4.0 stars (crowd wasn't emotionally invested)

### Compounding Effects

**Multiple Predictable Matches on Same Show:**

- First match: -5 rating penalty
- Second match: -7 penalty (crowd apathy building)
- Third match: -10 penalty (crowd dead)

**Result:** Show feels boring despite quality wrestling

## SURPRISE BOOKING (Pattern Breaking)

### When to Swerve

**Indicators:**

- Predictability Index > 60
- Crowd openly predicting outcomes
- Match ratings declining despite quality
- Fan Cynicism Index rising

**Swerve Options:**

- **Title change on TV** (when pattern is "never changes on TV")
- **Clean babyface win** (when pattern is "heel always screws them")
- **Contract signing without brawl** (when pattern is "always brawls")
- **Favorite loses clean** (when pattern is "Superman never loses")

**Timing:**

- Not every swerve must be huge
- Small pattern breaks maintain freshness
- Save big swerves for maximum-stake moments

### Swerve Frequency

**Optimal:** 1 major surprise every 2-3 months

- Too frequent: Feels random, no pattern to break
- Too rare: Predictability builds, crowd bored

---

## CLEAN FINISH VALUE

### When Screwy Finishes Are the Pattern

**Paradox:** If every finish is dirty, **clean finishes become surprises**

**Example Progression:**

1. PPV matches end screwy 5 times in a row (pattern)
2. Crowd expects interference, ref bump, DQ
3. Match 6: Clean finish, decisive winner
4. **Crowd Reaction:** Shocked, satisfied, "finally!"

**Strategic Clean Finishes:**

- After extended period of screwy finishes
- To establish new champion's legitimacy
- In grudge match blow-off (decisive ending)
- To reset crowd expectations

## FAN CYNICISM INDEX

### Accumulated Distrust in Booking

**Builds From:**

- Broken promises (teased pushes that never happen)
- Ignored crowd reactions (forcing Roman despite boos)
- Repetitive patterns without payoff
- False finishes overuse
- Authority abuse (evil boss storylines ad nauseam)

**Cynicism Scale:**

| Level | Crowd Attitude | Booking Challenge |
|-------|----------------|-------------------|
| **0-20** | Trusting | Booking works as intended |
| **21-40** | Skeptical | Need to deliver payoffs |
| **41-60** | Jaded | Must surprise to get reaction |
| **61-80** | Hostile | Crowd rejects booking preemptively |
| **81-100** | Dead | Cannot get anything over |

**Recovery:**

- Deliver on promises
- Listen to crowd reactions
- Surprise but logical booking
- Long-term storytelling payoffs
- Consistent quality over time

**Time Required:** 6-12 months minimum to rebuild trust
