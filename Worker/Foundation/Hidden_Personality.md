## HIDDEN PERSONALITY TRAITS (1-20 Scale)

| Trait | Description | Impact |
|-------|-------------|--------|
| **Ego** | Self-importance, need for recognition and respect | Booking compliance, main event pushes, tag team chemistry |
| **Loyalty** | Commitment to promotion/territory/tradition/faction | Contract negotiations, exodus risk, faction stability |
| **Morality** | Ethical boundaries, rule-following vs. chaos | Heel/face alignment believability, scandal risk, hardcore comfort |
| **Traditionalism** | Respect for wrestling history, resistance to change | Style adaptation, modern booking acceptance, gimmick changes |
| **Paranoia** | Distrust, perception of backstage politics against them | Interprets losses as burial, resists jobs, locker room tension |
| **Drive** | Ambition, work ethic, hunger for success | Training improvement rate, career longevity, comeback attempts |
| **Risk-Taking** | Willingness to do dangerous spots, career gambles | Hardcore match comfort, spot innovation, injury risk |
| **Resilience** | Emotional recovery from setbacks, toughness | Morale decay resistance, comeback arc potential |
| **Adaptability** | (May overlap with visible Mental stat) | Style changes, gimmick pivots, role evolution |

**Note:** Some traits (like Adaptability) may exist both as hidden personality trait AND visible mental skill, with different mechanical impacts.

| Trait | Description | Impact |
|-------|-------------|--------|
| **Coachability** | How well a worker listens, learns, absorbs training | Training improvement rate, skill development |

| Profile | What Happens |
|---------|-------------|
| High Drive + High Coachability | Rapid improvement: seeks training AND absorbs it |
| High Drive + Low Coachability | Works hard but repeats mistakes, stubborn |
| Low Drive + High Coachability | Can improve if pushed by others, won't seek it out |
| Low Drive + Low Coachability | Stagnates, resists change |

#### "Needs Rub" Hidden Flag (#159)

| Trait | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **Needs Rub** | Hidden flag marking workers who specifically need a big opponent to elevate them |

**Triggers:**
- When matched with established stars, Rub Mechanics activate
- DDP needed Savage; Kawada needed Misawa
- Some workers can self-elevate; others require external help

**Career Impact:**
- Workers with this flag may stall indefinitely without proper booking
- Smart bookers identify and provide rub opportunities
- Without rub, worker hits invisible ceiling regardless of skill

## ARCHETYPE MECHANICS

### Assignment

- Generated workers receive 1 primary archetype
- Optional secondary archetype for complexity
- Ranges allow individuality (two Ace Complex workers can still differ)

### Revelation

- Archetypes are **not displayed to player**
- Revealed through emergent behavior:
  - Contract negotiations (Mercenary vs. Loyal Soldier)
  - Booking reactions (Ace Complex refusing midcard)
  - Locker room incidents (Bombthrower stirring shit)
  - Career decisions (Ghost Walker sudden retirement)

### Evolution

- Archetypes can **shift over time** based on career events:
  - Chronic Underdog â†’ Ace Complex (after title run)
  - Innocent Idealist â†’ Backstage Politician (after betrayal)
  - Pressure Cooker â†’ Self-Destructive (burnout)
  - Loyal Soldier â†’ Mercenary Soul (contract dispute)

### Interaction with Visible Stats

- Hidden traits affect **decision-making and storylines**
- Visible skills affect **match performance**
- Example: High Ego + Low Mic Skills = wants spotlight but struggles to deliver promos

## BEHAVIORAL TRIGGERS

### High Ego (16-20)

- Demands main event booking
- Resists jobs to younger talent
- May refuse to put others over cleanly
- High risk of "going into business for themselves"

### Low Ego (1-6)

- Willing to job frequently
- Accepts midcard booking gracefully
- Good for putting others over
- Risk: becomes invisible, loses crowd connection

### High Loyalty (16-20)

- Stays with promotion through rough patches
- Defends promotion publicly
- Mentors younger talent
- Low exodus risk even with bad booking

### Low Loyalty (1-6)

- Jumps ship for better offers
- Leaks backstage info
- High exodus risk
- May form factions to gain leverage

### High Paranoia (16-20)

- Sees every loss as political burial
- Creates locker room tension
- Demands creative control clauses
- May refuse to work with certain opponents

### Low Paranoia (1-6)

- Trusts bookers completely
- Risk: gets exploited or buried without resistance

### High Risk-Taking (16-20)

- Requests hardcore/stipulation matches
- Does dangerous spots even when not needed
- High injury risk but crowd memorable
- May burn out faster

### Low Risk-Taking (1-6)

- Refuses hardcore bookings
- Conservative in-ring style
- Lower injury risk but less crowd pop

### High Drive (16-20)

- Trains constantly, skills improve faster
- Pushes through injuries (good and bad)
- Attempts comebacks even when past prime
- High burnout risk if overbooked

### Low Drive (1-6)

- Coasts on talent, skills plateau
- Retires early if not pushed
- Low training improvement rate

## PERSONALITY CONFLICTS

### Worker vs. Worker

**High Ego + High Ego** = Both want spotlight, resist putting each other over  
**High Traditionalism + Low Traditionalism** = Style clash, "wrong generation" heat  
**High Paranoia + Backstage Politician** = Paranoid worker sees real manipulation  
**Loyal Soldier + Mercenary Soul** = "Sellout" accusations

### Worker vs. Management

**High Ego + Midcard Booking** = Contract disputes, morale collapse  
**High Loyalty + Betrayal Angle** = Emotional resistance to turn  
**High Traditionalism + Modern Gimmick** = Refuses to adapt  
**High Paranoia + Any Job** = Sees burial, may no-show

### Positive Chemistry

**Martyr Spirit + Chronic Underdog** = Natural mentor relationship  
**Pressure Cooker + Pressure Cooker** = Epic high-stakes matches  
**Loyal Soldier + Loyal Soldier** = Reliable tag team chemistry  
**Bombthrower + Backstage Politician** = Drama content gold

## TRAIT EVOLUTION TRIGGERS

### Major Events That Shift Traits:

**Title Win:**

- Ego +2-4
- Drive +1-3
- Paranoia -1-2 (validation)

**Injury (Long-term):**

- Drive -2-4
- Paranoia +2-4
- Resilience test (grows or collapses)

**Betrayal (Turned on):**

- Loyalty -3-6
- Paranoia +4-8
- Morality shift (may embrace heel tactics)

**Burial (Extended Jobbing):**

- Ego -2-4
- Paranoia +3-6
- Drive may collapse or spike (varies by archetype)

**Contract Dispute:**

- Loyalty -2-5
- Ego +1-3
- May trigger archetype shift (Loyal Soldier â†’ Mercenary)

**Mentor Death/Retirement:**

- Traditionalism +2-4
- Drive varies (inspired or devastated)
- May trigger Ghost Walker tendencies

**Scandal:**

- Morality -2-6 (if guilty)
- Paranoia +3-8
- Loyalty to promotion tested

## DRIVE/GOAL SHIFT SYSTEM (#38)

ðŸ“Œ **Source:** Vol 1 Extraction #38, #68-70

### Core Concept

"Once goals are met (titles, respect, money), motivations mutate."

### Drive Attribute Effects

| Drive Level | Effect |
|-------------|--------|
| High Drive (16-20) | Constantly improves, seeks opportunities, fights through setbacks |
| Moderate Drive (10-15) | Plateaus at realistic level unless incentivized |
| Low Drive (6-9) | Coasts after fame, mentally checks out |
| Very Low Drive (1-5) | Collapses under success, "could have been" guys |

### Goal Shift Mechanics

| Scenario | Outcome |
|----------|----------|
| Chase dream achieved | May shift to "coast and vibe" mode |
| Post-title win, low Drive | Matches become shorter, safer, lazier |
| Major injury forces reinvention | Can trigger style shift (Onita, Danielson) |
| Intervention (new challenge, heel turn) | Can revive motivation |

### Example: Nakamura (Locked)

- Wins top title
- Drive stat naturally declines post-achievement
- Matches become shorter, safer, lazier
- Fans slowly feel it: "Nakamura still has star aura, but matches aren't what they used to be."
- Without intervention, coasts and fades from top draws

### Effort Visibility

Crowd and locker room can tell if worker is "mailing it in":
- Fans get restless over time
- Locker room loses respect
- Agents note it in reports

### Self-Driven Growth Traits (#68)

| Trait | What It Means |
|-------|---------------|
| Drive | How determined to improve, even when "good enough" |
| Self-Awareness | How accurately they judge own strengths/weaknesses |
| Adaptability | Willingness to shift style, learn new skills, take risks |
| Ego | How much they refuse help or constructive criticism |

### Growth Scenarios

| Profile | What Happens |
|---------|-------------|
| High Drive, High Self-Awareness, High Adaptability, Modest Ego | Actively seeks training, naturally improves skill gaps (Omega, late 90s Kawada) |
| High Drive, Low Self-Awareness, High Ego | Thinks they're better than they are, doesn't address real weaknesses |
| Low Drive, High Ego | Coasts on reputation, resents criticism |

### Events That Influence Growth (or Decay)

| Event | Impact |
|-------|--------|
| Major Loss/Embarrassment | Humbling can spark real growth if worker has adaptability |
| Serious Injury | Some accept decline; some reinvent (Onita, Danielson) |
| Major Feud Against Superior Opponent | High-Drive workers use feud to grow; low-Drive workers coast and get exposed |

## WORKER REFUSAL/RESISTANCE MECHANICS (#44)

ðŸ“Œ **Source:** Vol 1 Extraction #44

### Core Concept

Workers aren't chess pieces. They have opinions about their booking and will resist decisions they find unacceptable based on their personality traits.

### Resistance Examples

| Resistance Type | Example | Trigger |
|-----------------|---------|--------|
| **Job Resistance** | "Austin refuses to job clean under 15 minutes unless morale is 90+" | Low Ego threshold crossed |
| **Turn Resistance** | How hard a worker fights alignment changes | Traditionalism + Loyalty |
| **Push Resistance** | How workers resist being pushed down | Ego + Paranoia |
| **Internal Resistance Threshold** | When crossed, triggers events without player control | Multiple traits |

### Gimmick Pressure System

Workers trapped in wrong gimmicks actively push for change:

| Worker Satisfaction | Behavior |
|---------------------|----------|
| Happy with gimmick | Performs at peak |
| Tolerable gimmick | Slight performance dip |
| Unhappy with gimmick | Demands change, morale drops |
| Hates gimmick | Threatens to leave, sabotages performance |

**Example:** "Steve Austin hates Ringmaster, demands change."

**Resolution Options:**
- Grant gimmick change
- Explain reasoning (may or may not accept)
- Refuse (risk losing worker or poor performance)

### Creative Control

Top stars negotiate for title shots, creative control, money. Uses leverage based on overness:

| Overness Level | Leverage |
|----------------|----------|
| Megastar (90+) | Can demand almost anything |
| Main Eventer (75-89) | Significant negotiating power |
| Upper Midcard (60-74) | Some influence |
| Midcard and below | Take what you're given |

## WORKER COASTING / MAIL IT IN MODE (#117)

ðŸ“Œ **Source:** Vol 1 Extraction #117

### Core Concept

Workers with met goals and low Drive begin coasting. This isn't laziness per se, but the absence of fire that once drove them.

### Coasting Symptoms

| Indicator | Detection Method |
|-----------|------------------|
| Shorter matches | Match length decreases vs. historical average |
| Safer style | Fewer big bumps, less risk-taking |
| Reduced effort | Match quality dips |
| Phoning it in | Crowd senses lack of fire |

### Trigger Conditions

- Post-title run + Low Drive + No new challenge
- Major goal achieved + Drive naturally declining
- Injury recovery + lost hunger
- Contract security + no motivation

### Detection Methods

- Match quality metrics declining
- Road agent reports noting lack of effort
- Crowd reactions becoming tepid
- Locker room gossip about "checked out" worker

### Recovery Paths

| Intervention | Success Rate |
|--------------|-------------|
| New rival | High (competition revives fire) |
| Heel turn | Moderate-High (reinvention) |
| Reinvention angle | Moderate (if willing to change) |
| Contract ultimatum | Low (may just leave) |
| Personal crisis | Variable (can break or remake) |

### Why Separate From Kayfabe Stats

**Key Insight:** Haku could rip your face off but wasn't a cardio machine in ring. Bart Gunn won Brawl for All but was nothing special in kayfabe.
