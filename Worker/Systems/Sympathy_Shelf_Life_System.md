## Core Concept

You must be able to instruct workers to build sympathy around a specific affliction (limb, injury, emotional scar). BUT if it's overplayed:
- Crowds get fatigued, cynical, or hostile toward the "permanent" injury narrative
- True emotional resonance has a shelf life unless the story evolves or resolves
- Wrestling is an emotional craft, not mechanical repetition

**You can't just "sell the same knee forever."** Emotional weight must be earned and re-earned, or resolved meaningfully.

## What Happens at Breaking Point

When sympathy freshness crosses the critical threshold:

| Effect | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Crowd Sympathy Collapses** | Fans stop caring about the injury |
| **Becomes Meme** | "Oh, his bad back again lol" |
| **Worker Morale Damage** | Worker feels emotionally exposed for no payoff |
| **Match Emotional Weight Drops** | Sympathy selling no longer boosts match quality |

## Real-World Examples

| Storyline | Outcome |
|-----------|---------|
| **Tetsuya Naito's knee injuries** | Booked smartly: fans still emotionally react years later because injuries were tied to key losses, redemption arcs, character evolution |
| **Matt Jackson's back selling** | Became a meme because it was played the same way across too many matches without escalation or resolution |

**Emotion vs Repetition = survival or death of sympathy.**
