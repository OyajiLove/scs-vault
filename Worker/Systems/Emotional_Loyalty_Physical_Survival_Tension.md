## 1. TENSION CAUSES (4 FACTORS)

| Cause | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **Outlaw Spirit vs Self-Preservation** | Deep emotional bonds keep workers tied to dangerous promotions, even when physical survival would mean leaving |
| **Survivor Guilt Chains** | Workers feel abandoning the ship is betrayal. Staying loyal to promotions, trainers, or friends even as danger grows |
| **Career Rebirth vs Emotional Death** | Some workers risk career death to save physical health; others stay loyal and burn out emotionally or physically |
| **Fanbase Emotional Expectations** | Fans romanticize loyalty, emotionally punishing those who leave doomed promotions for survival |

---

## 2. WORKER CRISIS TYPES (4 PATHS)

| Crisis Type | Worker Behavior | Legacy Outcome |
|-------------|-----------------|----------------|
| **Self-Destructive Loyalty** | Worker stays loyal despite physical collapse. Career ends prematurely. | Romantically mythologized |
| **Bitter Survivalist** | Worker saves themselves but loses emotional fan loyalty. | Remembered as traitor by loyalist fans |
| **Redemptive Survivor** | Worker leaves ethically, preserving emotional authenticity. | Slow rehabilitation in public memory possible |
| **Secret Survivor** | Worker pretends loyalty while planning exit. | Complex arcs of guilt, relief, and betrayal |

### Path Probability by Personality

| Hidden Trait | Likely Crisis Path |
|--------------|-------------------|
| **High Loyalty + High Traditionalism** | Self-Destructive Loyalty |
| **Low Loyalty + High Drive** | Bitter Survivalist |
| **High Morality + Moderate Loyalty** | Redemptive Survivor |
| **High Paranoia + Moderate Ego** | Secret Survivor |

## 4. HISTORICAL MODELS

| Worker | Survival vs Loyalty Outcome |
|--------|----------------------------|
| **Sabu (Post-ECW Collapse)** | Stayed loyal to outlaw image but drifted physically broken. Romantic loyalty preserved. Self-Destructive Loyalty path. |
| **Chris Candido** | Emotional martyr of reckless loyalty culture. Tragic romantic myth solidified post-mortem. Ultimate Self-Destructive Loyalty. |
| **Taz (Jump to WWF)** | Mixed reaction. Respected by many for survival but emotionally "lost" among some hardcore loyalists. Redemptive Survivor path. |
| **Radicalz (WCW to WWF)** | Group departure softened individual blame. Redemptive Survivors through collective action. |

### Taz Case Study

**Context:** ECW star, physical toll accumulating, WWF opportunity

**Crisis Type:** Redemptive Survivor (attempted)

**Decision Factors:**
- Physical risk HIGH (neck injuries)
- Financial opportunity HIGH
- Loyalty to ECW HIGH
- Paul Heyman relationship strong

**Departure:** Public but respectful, acknowledged ECW's importance

**Fan Reaction:**
- Mainstream fans: Respected career move
- ECW hardcore: Mixed, some felt abandoned
- Long-term: Mostly rehabilitated, ECW legacy intact

**Lesson:** Authentic departure framing determines recovery speed. Taz's respect for ECW history prevented permanent "traitor" label.

## 6. PHILOSOPHY

> "Some stay and die legends. Some leave and live forgotten. The cruel truth is that wrestling rewards sacrifice until it kills you, then mythologizes the corpse. The workers who escape carry guilt. The workers who stay carry scars. There's no clean exit from an outlaw culture. SCS models this tension because it's the heart of wrestling's tragedy: the business demands loyalty it cannot repay."
