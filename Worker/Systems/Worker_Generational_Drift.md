## 1. DRIFT CAUSES (4 FACTORS)

| Cause | Description | Inheritance Effect |
|-------|-------------|-------------------|
| **Training Lineage** | Workers trained by legends of fallen eras inherit emotional philosophies. Adapt, preserve, or rebel. | HIGH (+/-25-40) |
| **Promotion Emotional Culture** | Workers growing up in promotions tied to dead eras absorb loyalty to artifacts, styles, booking philosophies | MEDIUM (+/-15-30) |
| **Fanbase Emotional Drift** | Workers themselves shaped by fan narratives about what eras meant. Myth reinforcement or myth rejection. | MEDIUM (+/-10-25) |
| **Cultural Mood Shifts** | Political/social moods change what emotional stories workers want to tell (rebellion vs stability vs trauma arcs) | VARIABLE |

## 2. REACTION TYPES (4 CATEGORIES)

| Reaction Type | Emotional Behavior | Career Expression |
|---------------|-------------------|-------------------|
| **True Disciples** | Loyally preserve old philosophies, seeking to emotionally relive dead eras | Style preservation, tribute work, traditionalist booking |
| **Adaptive Evolutionaries** | Honor old emotional truths but reinvent storytelling styles for modern needs | Innovation within tradition, bridge-building |
| **Bitter Rejectors** | Emotionally reject fallen myths entirely, building identity around rebellion and negation | Anti-nostalgia, iconoclasm, new movement building |
| **Lost Romantics** | Paralyzed by nostalgia for eras they never truly lived. Emotional gaps in career arcs. | Imitation without understanding, aesthetic without substance |

### Reaction Type by Training Background

| Trainer Type | Likely Reaction |
|--------------|-----------------|
| **Living Legend (traditionalist)** | True Disciple or Lost Romantic |
| **Living Legend (innovative)** | Adaptive Evolutionary |
| **Modern Star** | Adaptive Evolutionary or Bitter Rejector |
| **No Strong Lineage** | Variable (cultural mood dominant) |

## 6. PHILOSOPHY

> "Every generation inherits the ghosts of the one before. Some workers become disciples, carrying the torch with reverence. Some become innovators, honoring the spirit while changing the form. Some become rebels, burning down everything their teachers built. And some become romantics, frozen in love with an era they never knew, imitating without understanding. How you inherit determines what you become."

Worker generational drift is how wrestling's philosophy transmits (or fails to transmit) across time.
