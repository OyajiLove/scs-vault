## 1. SURVIVOR CAUSES (4 TRIGGERS)

| Cause | Description | Trauma Severity |
|-------|-------------|-----------------|
| **Surviving Betrayals** | Workers surviving internal purges, Golden Era collapses, locker room civil wars. Emotionally scarred but still active. | HIGH |
| **Grief and Legacy Burden** | Workers tied to fallen figures or eras wrestle with survivor guilt, loyalty crises, and fan expectations | MEDIUM-HIGH |
| **Fanbase Abandonment or Investment** | Workers surviving failed promotions may lose fanbases completely or carry cult loyalists into new myths | VARIABLE |
| **Corporate Exploitation** | Surviving workers sometimes exploited as mascots for hollow revivals, or rebel against it to forge authentic redemption arcs | MEDIUM-HIGH |

## 2. SURVIVOR PATHS (4 TYPES)

| Survivor Type | Emotional Arc | Career Trajectory |
|---------------|---------------|-------------------|
| **Burned-Out Survivor** | Cannot escape emotional scars. Career spirals into bitterness, disillusionment, or aimless drift. | Decline without intervention |
| **Tragic Loyalist** | Clings to dead ideals too long. Slowly fades as world moves on emotionally without them. | Slow fade, cult following possible |
| **Redemptive Phoenix** | Accepts loss, pain, and forges a new emotional era by transforming grief into living mythology. | Major success potential |
| **Rebel Builder** | Actively rejects dead Golden Eras. Creates emotional counter-myths that redefine future wrestling memory. | High risk, high reward |

### Path Determination Factors

| Hidden Stat | Path Tendency |
|-------------|---------------|
| **High Drive + High Morality** | Redemptive Phoenix |
| **High Traditionalism + Low Drive** | Tragic Loyalist |
| **Low Morality + Low Drive** | Burned-Out Survivor |
| **Low Traditionalism + High Drive** | Rebel Builder |

## 3. SIMULATION MECHANICS

| Mechanic | Behavior |
|----------|----------|
| **Trauma Scarring** | Surviving betrayal, grief, false idol collapse leaves hidden loyalty/morale scars that linger for years unless actively healed |
| **Career Redemption Arc Triggering** | If booked into emotionally redemptive arcs (overcoming betrayal, healing divisions, building new legacies), survivors can rebuild loyalty, morale, fanbase trust |
| **Fanbase Refuge Loyalty** | Some fans stay emotionally loyal to survivor workers. Cult fanbases build organically around authentic pain arcs. |
| **Collapse Risk** | If emotional support fails, survivors spiral: morale crashes, loyalty decays, hidden mental fatigue builds |

### Trauma Scar Duration

| Trauma Source | Base Duration | Healing Modifier |
|---------------|---------------|------------------|
| **Minor Betrayal** | 1-2 years | -50% with redemption arc |
| **Major Betrayal** | 3-5 years | -40% with redemption arc |
| **Golden Era Collapse** | 5-10 years | -30% with redemption arc |
| **Mentor/Friend Death** | 5-15 years | -25% with tribute arc |
| **False Idol Failure** | 3-7 years | -35% with reinvention |

### Healing Mechanisms

| Mechanism | Healing Effect |
|-----------|----------------|
| **Redemption Arc (successful)** | -30% to -50% trauma duration |
| **Tribute Match/Show** | -10% to -20% trauma, +15 morale |
| **New Promotion Fresh Start** | -20% to -30% trauma |
| **Fanbase Rally** | +20 morale, -15% trauma |
| **Time Passage** | -5% per year (natural decay) |

### Hangman Page Case Study

**Trauma Source:** Elite fractures, Brawl Out fallout, booking inconsistency

**Survivor Path:** Redemptive Phoenix

**Arc Elements:**
- Self-doubt storylines (authentic to real situation)
- Anxiety/imposter syndrome themes
- Championship victory as emotional catharsis
- Fan investment in genuine struggle

**Success Factors:**
- Booking leaned into real emotions
- Fanbase refuge loyalty strong
- Time given for arc to develop
- Authentic character, not manufactured

**Lesson:** Redemptive Phoenix path requires booking that trusts the survivor's real pain and gives time for authentic healing narratives.

The phoenix doesn't just survive the fire. It becomes the fire. That's the difference between a tragic ending and a legendary beginning."
