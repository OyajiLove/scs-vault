## 1. BURDEN CAUSES (4 TRIGGERS)

| Cause | Description | Pressure Generation |
|-------|-------------|---------------------|
| **Direct ProtÃ©gÃ© Connection** | Trained by fallen legends, debuted during dying Golden Eras, emotional successors tied to mythic figures | HIGH (+3-5 per year) |
| **Promotion Identity Weight** | Workers representing promotions known for fallen styles or cultures, forced to embody or reinvent them under pressure | MEDIUM (+2-4 per year) |
| **Public Fanbase Expectations** | Fans project emotional loyalty onto new workers, demanding they "live up" to ghosts of the past | MEDIUM (+2-3 per year) |
| **Internal Worker Guilt/Grief** | Workers themselves feel internalized pressure to honor mentors, fallen eras, or styles. Pride, loyalty, resentment, or burnout. | VARIABLE (+1-5 based on personality) |

## 2. BURDEN TYPES (4 RESPONSES)

| Type | Emotional Effect | Career Trajectory |
|------|------------------|-------------------|
| **Protector of Myth** | Emotionally dedicated to honoring legacy. Loyalty high, storytelling arcs reflect genuine reverence. | Stable but potentially limited by reverence |
| **Rebel Against Myth** | Emotionally exhausted by legacy weight. Chooses to redefine or burn old emotional ties. | High risk, high reward. Can forge new identity or alienate fans. |
| **Crushed By Myth** | Collapses under weight of expectations. Spiraling into morale decay, loyalty drift, career burnout. | Tragic trajectory without intervention |
| **Myth Forging Survivor** | Channels grief into new mythology. Becomes bridge between dead era and living future. | Most successful long-term path, but rare |

## 4. FAN EXPECTATION PRESSURE

The bigger the emotional vacuum, the harsher expectations:

| Fallen Figure Tier | Expectation Pressure |
|--------------------|---------------------|
| **Immortal Icon** | EXTREME (+5 per year) |
| **Main Event Legend** | VERY HIGH (+4 per year) |
| **Beloved Upper-Midcarder** | HIGH (+3 per year) |
| **Cult Favorite** | MEDIUM (+2 per year) |
| **Regional/Niche Legend** | LOW (+1 per year) |

### Expectation Gap Calculation

```
Expectation_Gap = (Fallen_Figure_Mythological_Score - Worker_Current_Status) / 10

IF Expectation_Gap > 5: Pressure multiplier = 1.5x
IF Expectation_Gap > 8: Pressure multiplier = 2.0x
```

## 7. HISTORICAL MODELS

| Worker | Legacy Burden |
|--------|---------------|
| **Go Shiozaki (NOAH Post-Misawa)** | Protector of Myth. Emotionally attached to preserving King's Road style and Misawa's legacy at personal career cost. Noble but limiting. |
| **Hangman Adam Page (AEW Post-Elite Era)** | Myth Forging Survivor. Forged new emotional mythology through failure, grief, and redemption arcs. Model success case. |
| **David Flair (WCW Late 90s)** | Crushed By Myth. Overwhelmed and miscast by being pushed into father's legacy spotlight prematurely. Never recovered. |
| **Charlotte Flair** | Mixed. Oscillates between Protector and Rebel. Legacy burden visible in booking and public discourse. |
| **Randy Orton** | Myth Forger (eventually). Early career Crushed tendencies, evolved into own legend. Long timeline. |

### David Flair Case Study

**Burden Source:** Ric Flair's legendary status

**Pressure Factors:**
- Immediate family connection (maximum)
- Same promotion (WCW late era)
- Physical/skill gap (enormous)
- Premature push (no development time)
- Public comparisons (constant)

**Burden Type:** Crushed By Myth

**Career Trajectory:**
- Initial push: pressure accumulation
- Fan rejection: pressure spike
- Repackaging attempts: failed
- Career decline: predictable result
- Legacy: cautionary tale

**Lesson:** Legacy burden without support or realistic expectations destroys careers. The system must model this.

Some burn it all down to find themselves. And a rare few channel the grief into something new, something that honors the dead while giving life to the future. The burden is real.
