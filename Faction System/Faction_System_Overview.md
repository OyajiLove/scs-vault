## Core Concept

Factions are defined not just by who is in them, but by:
- **Why** those people are there
- **What** they represent emotionally
- **What purpose** they serve inside the group hierarchy
- **What story** the faction is meant to tell

Factions are emotional engines, not roster pages.

## Faction Roles âœ… LOCKED

| Role | Description | Booking Impact |
|------|-------------|----------------|
| **Leader** | Symbol of the faction's soul | Loss, betrayal, or turn = identity crisis. Title target. Often closing segment focus. |
| **Lieutenant / Workhorse** | Ring general. Defends faction in matches. | May carry belts or take lead when leader won't. Stable emotional core. Betrayal from here feels earned. |
| **Mouthpiece / Politician** | Speaks, manipulates, sets agendas. May not be top worker. | Influences booking angles. May negotiate for faction, speak post-match. |
| **Enforcer / Muscle** | Scary presence. May not win titles. Exists to impose fear. | Booked in protection matches, big bump finishes, visual dominance. |
| **ProtÃ©gÃ© / Future** | Younger or "adopted" member meant to carry the torch. | Push potential. Fans track their growth. Betrayals from here = fire. |
| **Fodder / Loyal Cannon Fodder** | Low-tier ally used in tags, backstage angles, morale buffers. | They eat pins. They get jumped. They build sympathy (or mockery). |
| **Ghost / Shadow Ally** | Appears rarely. May be only partially aligned. | Adds mystique, tension, long-term betrayal angles. Often the surprise turn or ace in the hole. Long-term memory bomb. |

**Roles can change over time, but emotional memory sticks.**

**AutoBooker understands this. It doesn't just reshuffle names.**

### When Faction CAN'T Fracture

| Condition | Result |
|-----------|--------|

**Protects Joshi factions, traditional dojo units, and sports-based trios from random TEW-style implosions.**
