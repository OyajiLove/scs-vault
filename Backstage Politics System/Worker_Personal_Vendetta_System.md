## Core Concept

When loyalty fractures, betrayals cut deep, or perceived injustices pile up, workers don't just passively lose morale. Sometimes personal vendettas form.

Vendettas are hidden emotional scars that:
- Create backstage sabotage risks
- Influence booking politics
- Spark factional warfare
- Cause match breakdowns, botches, sandbagging
- Leak political warfare into media indirectly
- Shape career survival (or collapse)

**Vendettas can live quietly for years, or erupt into open locker room wars when conditions align.**

---

## How Vendettas Are Created âœ… LOCKED

| Trigger | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Severe Loyalty Betrayal** | Close friends, tag teams, stablemates turned on each other in booking or backstage with no emotional closure |
| **Booking Injustice** | Workers buried unjustly (pushed aside for golden children, scapegoated post-scandal) hold silent grudge |
| **Backstage Sabotage** | Political power plays succeed: star wrestlers burying rising talents, match manipulations, sabotage angles |
| **Career-Wrecking Injury** | Workers blaming others (fairly or unfairly) for injuries that destroyed pushes or careers |
| **Soul Betrayal (Promotion Identity Drift)** | Workers emotionally bonded to original soul of promotion feel betrayed when company sells out emotionally (e.g., rebellion fed becoming corporate sellout) |

Vendettas aren't automatic. They form based on personality traits, emotional loyalty levels, betrayal severity, and world climate.

## Personality Traits that Influence Vendetta Risk âœ… LOCKED

| Trait | Effect |
|-------|--------|
| **High Ego** | Higher likelihood of taking career insults personally and forming vendettas |
| **High Paranoia** | More likely to perceive betrayal even when unintended, higher vendetta formation chance |
| **High Drive** | More likely to quietly survive and weaponize vendetta into comeback arcs later |
| **Low Loyalty** | May form vendettas but lack emotional follow-through to act on them consistently |

Different workers react differently to the same betrayals.

## How Vendettas Manifest Over Time âœ… LOCKED

| Stage | Behavior |
|-------|----------|
| **1. Quiet Resistance** | Workers subtly undermine pushes (half-hearted matches, sandbagging, leaking backstage discontent) |
| **2. Factional Splits** | Workers cluster loyalty around vendetta targets, invisible backstage alliances form or harden |
| **3. Match Sabotages** | If booked against vendetta targets, risk of match breakdowns (botches, no-selling, stiffness) increases |
| **4. Public Escalation** | If media starts sniffing rumors, vendettas can leak into public, destabilizing booking even if official stories stay controlled |
| **5. Career Sabotage** | Workers with political allies can sabotage vendetta targets' pushes silently through agents, booking suggestions, locker room whisper networks |

Vendettas live invisibly first but shape locker room power webs deeply over years.

## Real-World Examples

| Example | SCS Behavior |
|---------|--------------|
| **Bret Hart vs Shawn Michaels (Post-1996)** | Mutual vendettas poisoned locker room atmosphere, infected booking memory, climaxed violently |
| **Tetsuya Naito vs NJPW Management (Pre-LIJ)** | Naito's perceived betrayal by management created a silent vendetta arc, rebuilt into rebellion hero |
| **Kawada Loyalty to Baba vs Misawa Exodus** | Deep backstage vendetta emotional drift, altered career arcs for a decade |

**Vendettas aren't just feuds. They are invisible career scars that warp matches, pushes, retirements, and company survival.**
