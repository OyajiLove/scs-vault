## 1. WHEN EXILED FACTIONS FORM

| Trigger | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Clique Crushed in Backstage War** | Booking control lost, allies fired, political exile finalized |
| **Mass Firing or Forced Exodus** | A wave of workers booted or marginalized simultaneously: too strong to simply scatter |
| **Fanbase Sympathy Residuals** | Exiled workers retain emotional loyalty from fragments of the fanbase |
| **Media/Dirt Sheet Fueling** | Scandal, injustice, or visible booking sabotage narratives leak into media, creating martyr myths |
| **World Cultural Opportunity** | If cultural mood supports rebellion (indie boom eras, anti-corporate feelings), exiled factions find soil to plant seeds |

### Formation Thresholds

| Factor | Threshold |
|--------|-----------|
| **Minimum Clique Size** | 3+ workers with combined popularity 150+ |
| **Fanbase Residual** | 20%+ of original fanbase sympathetic to exiles |
| **Media Sympathy** | Positive or neutral media narrative |
| **Cultural Moment** | Anti-establishment sentiment high OR indie boom active |
| **Star Power Requirement** | At least 1 main event level worker |

---

## 2. PATHS OF EXILED FACTION EVOLUTION (4 PATHS)

| Path | Outcome | Requirements |
|------|---------|--------------|
| **New Promotion Formation** | Exiles band together to form indie feds, outlaw promotions, or splinter territories | Financial backing + 5+ workers + star power |
| **Rebel Brand Invasion** | Exiles sign en masse with rival promotion: form subculture inside enemy gates | Rival promotion interest + clique cohesion |
| **Nomadic Collective** | Exiles form traveling groups working multiple indie promotions without creating formal company | 3+ workers + indie circuit access |
| **Cult Movement Building** | Exiles become figureheads of fan protest movements ("We are real wrestling!" vs "corporate shills") | High media sympathy + passionate fanbase fragment |

### Path Selection Factors

| Factor | Favored Path |
|--------|-------------|
| **High Financial Backing** | New Promotion Formation |
| **Rival Promotion Hungry for Talent** | Rebel Brand Invasion |
| **Low Resources but High Passion** | Nomadic Collective |
| **Strong Ideological Message** | Cult Movement Building |
| **Mixed Factors** | Multiple paths possible simultaneously |

## 3. HISTORICAL MODELS

| Event | Path | Outcome |
|-------|------|---------|
| **The Radicalz to WWF (2000)** | Rebel Brand Invasion | Benoit, Guerrero, Malenko, Saturn exodus from WCW after political exile. Mixed success: some became legends, some faded. |
| **Bullet Club Early Formation** | Cult Movement Building ‚Üí New Brand | Foreigners in NJPW feeling political friction form outsider "gang" turning into global brand and merchandise phenomenon |
| **AEW Formation (2018-2019)** | New Promotion Formation | Indie and New Japan-tied exiles rally around Khan funding to create major alternative to WWE hegemony |
| **ROH 2002-2006 Core** | New Promotion Formation | Purist exiles from indies built technical workrate revolution against WWE crash TV culture |
| **TNA Formation (2002)** | New Promotion Formation | WCW refugees + Jarrett family create alternative. Mixed long-term success. |
| **ECW Original (1994-2001)** | Cult Movement Building | Exiles and misfits create ideological alternative. Massive cultural impact despite financial struggles. |

### AEW Case Study

**Trigger:** Elite (Kenny Omega, Young Bucks) felt undervalued by WWE system. Cody Rhodes exiled from WWE. ROH unable to capitalize.

**Path:** New Promotion Formation with Khan financial backing.

**Composition:** Star power (Omega, Jericho), media sympathy (Being The Elite), cultural moment (fan frustration with WWE monopoly).

**Outcome:** Major success. Created genuine alternative. Several exiles became Era Stars or Immortal Icons in new context.

## 4. SIMULATION MECHANICS

| Mechanic | Behavior |
|----------|----------|
| **Clique Size and Composition** | Determines initial strength of new movement. Star power, charisma, loyalty levels matter. |
| **Fanbase Residual Loyalty** | Exiled workers start with emotional base: size and anger level determine movement momentum |
| **Media Sympathy** | Early media narrative ("righteous rebels" vs "bitter losers") hugely impacts public adoption |
| **Financial Backing Roll** | New promotions may rise or fall based on financial health checks: TV deals, indie circuits, crowdfunded survival |
| **Exile Solidarity Bonus** | Shared suffering creates loyalty: exiled cliques have +20% internal cohesion for first 2 years |

### New Promotion Viability Check

| Factor | Weight |
|--------|--------|
| **Financial Backing** | 30% |
| **Star Power** | 25% |
| **Media/Fan Momentum** | 20% |
| **Cultural Timing** | 15% |
| **Operational Competence** | 10% |

### Viability Score Outcomes

| Score | Outcome |
|-------|---------|
| **0-30** | Failed launch or quick collapse |
| **31-50** | Small indie with limited growth |
| **51-70** | Regional success, potential for growth |
| **71-85** | National impact, TV deal possible |
| **86-100** | Major alternative, industry-reshaping |

## 5. ADDITIONAL MECHANICS

### Long-Term Rivalry Memory

Promotions born out of exile often remain locked into mythic feuds with origin promotion for decades (even if ownership changes).

| Mechanic | Behavior |
|----------|----------|
| **Origin Myth Recording** | World memory forever records "founded by exiled loyalists of..." |
| **Hijack Risk** | If exiled faction hijacks rival promotion ‚Üí Risk of new civil wars, factional infighting, booking chaos |
| **Failure Path** | If exiled faction fails ‚Üí workers fade into indie wilderness, "Lost Legend" paths multiplied |

### Growth Factors vs Collapse Risks

| Growth Factors | Collapse Risks |
|----------------|----------------|
| Cultural wave match | Early financial starvation |
| Strong initial storytelling | Internal ego splits |
| Emotional leadership (worker charisma) | Fanbase fractures post-honeymoon |
| Clear identity ("purist," "rebellious," "dangerous") | Over-promise, under-deliver perception |

### NOAH Exodus Model (Core Historical Reference)

| Element | Details |
|---------|--------|
| **Event** | NOAH Formation (2000) |
| **Exiles** | Misawa, Kobashi, Taue, Akiyama, most of AJPW locker room |
| **Trigger** | Political war with Motoko Baba after Giant Baba's death |
| **Movement Outcome** | Entire new promotion born in rebellion. Emotional loyalty migrated en masse. |
| **Original Brand Effect** | AJPW decimated. Needed nearly 15 years to partially recover. |
| **Emotional Layer** | Fans saw NOAH as "true AJPW spirit": memory fracture lasted generations |

## 6. REBEL BRAND INVASION MECHANICS

When exiles join a rival promotion en masse:

| Mechanic | Behavior |
|----------|----------|
| **Internal Subculture Formation** | Exiles form clique within new promotion |
| **Booking Tension** | Original roster may resent invaders |
| **Fan Segment Import** | Exiles bring their fans with them |
| **Ideological Clash** | Exile philosophy vs new promotion philosophy |
| **Eventual Integration or Conflict** | Either assimilate or trigger new power struggle |

### Invasion Success Factors

| Factor | Effect |
|--------|--------|
| **New Promotion Welcomes Outsiders** | +30% success |
| **Exiles Willing to Adapt** | +20% success |
| **Original Roster Hostile** | -25% success |
| **Booking Favors Invaders** | +15% success short-term, -20% long-term (resentment) |

---

## 6. CULT MOVEMENT BUILDING MECHANICS

When exiles become ideological figureheads:

| Mechanic | Behavior |
|----------|----------|
| **"Real Wrestling" Narrative** | Exiles claim to represent authentic wrestling vs corporate product |
| **Fan Protest Rallying** | Disaffected fans rally to exile cause |
| **Merch and Media Empire** | Movement can generate revenue without formal promotion |
| **Cultural Impact vs Financial Success** | Cult movements often have outsized cultural impact relative to size |
| **Eventual Formalization** | Successful cult movements may evolve into promotions (ECW, AEW) |

---

## 7. EXILE OUTCOMES

### For Individual Workers

| Outcome | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Era Star in New Context** | Exile leads to legendary career elsewhere (Cody Rhodes) |
| **Lost Legend** | Exile ends promising career (Marty Jannetty post-Rockers) |
| **Cult Hero** | Smaller scale but passionate following (many ECW originals) |
| **Bitter Footnote** | Fades into obscurity, remembered only for what-ifs |
| **Triumphant Return** | Eventually returns to original promotion as conquering hero |

### For Promotions Created by Exiles

| Outcome | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Industry Reshaper** | Creates genuine alternative (AEW, ECW) |
| **Regional Success** | Sustainable but limited (many territories) |
| **Flash in Pan** | Initial buzz fades quickly |
| **Absorbed by Original** | Bought out or talent raided back |
| **Legacy Brand** | Fails but remembered fondly (ECW post-2001) |
