## Core Concept

> "The booker doesn't work alone. Sometimes they can't. Sometimes they're watched. Sometimes the committee is smarter. Sometimes it's the reason everything dies."

The Booking Committee System models internal power structures that:
- Share power across a committee
- Host internal debates or conflicts
- Make booking a negotiation or battlefield
- Let the player choose their level of control (or submission)

---

## Committee Types âœ… LOCKED

| Type | Description |
|------|-------------|
| **Solo Booker (Default)** | One booker with full authority. May consult others but has final say. Committee exists in name only. |
| **Consultative Committee** | Booker makes decisions but committee members influence heavily (advice, push preferences) |
| **Voting Committee** | Every major booking decision requires a vote: title changes, pushes, faction betrayals, signings/firings |
| **Split Committee** | Each member controls a division or roster (juniors, women, heavyweights, tag). Booker oversees arc flow. |
| **Power Vacuum** | No clear head. Votes sway wildly. Clashes frequent. AI feds with this may self-destruct or surprise everyone. |

## Freeform Input in Booking Meetings (Vol 5 line 1164) âœ… LOCKED

Players can type dialogue to suggest booking ideas or respond to others in creative meetings, not just pick from presets.

**How It Works:**
- Player can suggest segments, angles, or match logic in real-time using freeform text
- Engine interprets input based on:
  - **Tone** (supportive, egotistical, manipulative, sarcastic)
  - **Booking logic match** (does it align with promotion DNA, worker arcs?)
  - **Respect memory** (does the committee trust your vision?)
  - **Style compatibility** (pitching hardcore to a "soul purist" fed?)

**Committee responds dynamically:**
- Support, resistance, laughter, resentment, cold silence
- Agent interjections and factional bias all kick in

**Example reactions:**
- "You're booking another betrayal? That's three in six weeks."
- "You trying to lose the crowd or win a slap fight on Twitter?"

**Consequence Memory:**
- How you phrase suggestions is remembered
- Burn bridges with passive-aggressive/dismissive language = votes turn against you
- Show strong vision and adaptability = gain soft power, convert rivals

**Committee meetings become interactive narrative hubs, not just voting systems.**
