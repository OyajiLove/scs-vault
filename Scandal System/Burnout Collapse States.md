## Overview

When a promotion tries to survive too many scandals, shields too many toxic workers, and ignores rot too long, it doesn't just lose some pop. It collapses emotionally, financially, and politically.

**Core Truth:** You can get away with protecting one scandal. Maybe even two if you're huge and lucky. But if you keep enabling, the whole system rots from the inside out.

---

## Burnout Triggers

| Trigger | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Accumulated Scandals** | Number of exposed or suppressed scandals hits critical mass |
| **Locker Room Morale** | Drops below critical point (e.g., 30% = danger zone) |
| **Sponsor Loss** | Major sponsor exodus triggers panic at leadership level |
| **Fan Loyalty** | Promotion loyalty drops dangerously low |
| **Shield Breaks** | Fan/media/public tolerance cracks after one scandal too many |
| **Media Entrenchment** | Ongoing investigations keep scandals in spotlight |

---

## Burnout Symptoms

| Symptom | Effect |
|---------|--------|
| **Mass Worker Exodus** | Top talent and midcarders seek releases or no-show events |
| **TV Deal Deterioration** | Worse time slots, contract renegotiations at lower value, cancellation threats |
| **Sponsor Withdrawals** | Merch deals, endorsements, brand tie-ins crumble |
| **Fanbase Collapse** | Attendance nosedives, online engagement plummets, merch sales collapse |
| **Locker Room Backstabbing** | Political assassinations, sabotage, shoot comments skyrocket |
| **Rise of New Promotions** | Rivals poach top stars and market as "clean alternatives" |

---

## Burnout Stages

| Stage | Status | Description |
|-------|--------|-------------|
| **Warning Signs** | 0-20% decay | Minor sponsor losses, worker grumbling, sporadic fan backlash |
| **Structural Instability** | 21-40% decay | Talent leaves mid-card ranks; revenue down 10-25% |
| **Severe Crisis** | 41-60% decay | TV negotiations hostile; locker room fractures into cliques; new scandals emerge organically |
| **Death Spiral** | 61-80% decay | Booking chaotic; morale sub-20%; no new signings; stars threaten lawsuits; fans treat promotion as dead brand |
| **Collapse Event** | 81-100% decay | Promotion folds, gets absorbed by rival, or reboots under new ownership |

---

## Recovery Options

| Action | Effect |
|--------|--------|
| **Purge Toxic Figures** | Fire scandal figures even if painful short-term |
| **Transparent Reforms** | Publicly overhaul backstage culture; PR campaigns about safety, diversity, new leadership |
| **Anchor Top Talent** | Secure loyalty of surviving beloved top stars |
| **Reformer Leadership** | Hire GMs/bookers with "Clean Hands" or "Worker First" tags |
| **Relinquish Power** | Owner steps back; hires GMs; goes owner-only to save perception |
