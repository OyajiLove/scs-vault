## 1. FORMATION CAUSES (4 TRIGGERS)

| Cause | Description | Formation Strength |
|-------|-------------|-------------------|
| **Emotional Rebellion** | Workers dissatisfied with dominant booking, emotional dishonesty, or corporate rot break away to forge new emotional homes | HIGH |
| **Exile Movements** | Fired, blackballed, or politically frozen workers band together, forging survival promotions from emotional loyalty and necessity | MEDIUM-HIGH |
| **Artistic Vision Craving** | Workers craving emotional storytelling purity, hardcore honesty, or true rebellion form micro-promotions centered on philosophy, not profit | MEDIUM |
| **Fanbase Underground Support** | Disillusioned fans migrate emotionally to support underground shows, breathing life into micro-movements | MEDIUM |

## 5. HISTORICAL MODELS

| Micro Promotion | Emotional Outcome |
|-----------------|-------------------|
| **FMW (Atsushi Onita 1989)** | Deathmatch, raw emotional realism, rebellion against puroresu prestige. Became legendary, spawned entire subgenre. |
| **ROH (2002)** | Pure wrestling philosophy rebellion against crash TV aftermath. Grew into sustainable niche, then sold to major. |
| **GCW (Modern)** | Emotional deathmatch storytelling and ironic cultural rebellion hybrid. Currently sustainable niche with cult following. |
| **CHIKARA** | Ironic/meta wrestling rebellion. Sustainable niche for years, then dramatic collapse. Romantic myth status. |

### ECW Case Study

**Formation:**
- 1992 as Eastern Championship Wrestling
- 1994 rebranded as Extreme Championship Wrestling
- Paul Heyman's artistic vision

**Type:** Hardcore Haven + Purist Elements

**Survival Factors:**
- Emotional resonance: VERY HIGH (cult audience)
- Financial stability: LOW (chronic money issues)
- Cultural memory: VERY HIGH (defined an era)
- Talent pipeline: EXCEPTIONAL (produced dozens of stars)

**Outcome:** Collapse (2001) + Legendary Myth Status

**Lesson:** Financial instability can kill even culturally essential promotions. But the myth lives forever.

## 8. PHILOSOPHY

> "Revolutions are born in forgotten halls. Not in arenas, but in bingo halls, warehouses, and backyards. Workers who believe in something different, fans who are hungry for something real, and just enough money to keep the lights on. Most micro-promotions die in obscurity. But some become ROH. Some become ECW. Some become the seed that grows into AEW. You never know which small rebellion will change everything."

Micro-promotions are wrestling's R&D labs. SCS models them as high-risk, high-reward cultural experiments.
