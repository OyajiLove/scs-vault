## 1. FORMATION CAUSES (4 DRIVERS)

| Cause | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **Worker Exiles** | Fired, exiled, or politically frozen workers band together seeking artistic freedom or revenge |
| **Fanbase Disillusionment** | Fans loyal to old emotional ideals seek a new home, or build one with indie organizers |
| **Philosophical Vacuum** | World changes but no major promotion adapts: new voices emerge to fill the gap |
| **Survivor Promotions Rotting** | Survivors embrace nostalgia/rot: new movements driven by genuine innovation or rebellion form outside |

---

## 2. MOVEMENT TYPES (4 PATHS)

| Type | Description | Historical Examples |
|------|-------------|---------------------|
| **Purist Revolution** | Focused on "real wrestling," in-ring mastery, emotional storytelling revival | ROH 2002, early NOAH |
| **Spectacle Revolution** | Focused on cinematic presentation, over-the-top storytelling, or edgy reinvention | Early Lucha Underground |
| **Outlaw Anarchy** | Focused on total defiance of norms: deathmatches, hardcore, satire, self-awareness | FMW, early ECW, GCW |
| **Emotional Realism Wave** | Focused on deep emotional arcs, mental health storytelling, generational trauma: realism-heavy arcs | AEW Hangman arc, modern joshi emotional storytelling |

### Movement Type Indicators

| Type | Core Appeal | Target Fanbase |
|------|-------------|----------------|
| **Purist Revolution** | "This is REAL wrestling" | Workrate fetishists, purists, historians |
| **Spectacle Revolution** | "Wrestling as art/cinema" | Creative fans, mainstream crossover |
| **Outlaw Anarchy** | "F**k the system" | Rebels, ironists, thrill-seekers |
| **Emotional Realism** | "Wrestling that FEELS real" | Story-focused fans, emotional investors |

## 3. SIMULATION MECHANICS

| Mechanic | Behavior |
|----------|----------|
| **Seed Composition Check** | New movement viability based on: exiled worker charisma, fan loyalty clusters, financial seed roll |
| **Emotional Core Assignment** | Every new movement must emotionally define itself: Rebellion? Restoration? Escapism? Anarchy? |
| **Migration Pull Strength** | Movements pull fanbases based on how closely they emotionally mirror displaced needs |
| **Historical Memory Assignment** | Success or failure deeply affects world memory (romantic martyrs, betrayal cults, cultural saviors) |

### Viability Check Factors

| Factor | Weight |
|--------|--------|
| **Worker Star Power** | 25% |

| **Fan Loyalty Cluster Size** | 20% |

### Viability Score Outcomes

| Score | Outcome |
|-------|---------|
| **0-30** | Movement fails to launch or collapses within months |
| **31-50** | Small cult following, limited regional impact |
| **51-70** | Sustainable movement with growth potential |
| **71-85** | Major cultural impact, potential industry reshaper |

## 4. EMOTIONAL CORE ASSIGNMENT

Every new movement must define its emotional center:

| Core | Description | Fan Appeal |
|------|-------------|------------|
| **Rebellion** | Against corporate wrestling, against the machine | Anti-establishment fans, young rebels |
| **Restoration** | Return to "true" wrestling values | Purists, nostalgic fans, historians |
| **Escapism** | Fantasy, spectacle, otherworldly presentation | Creative fans, casual viewers |
| **Anarchy** | Chaos, violence, defiance of all norms | Thrill-seekers, ironists, outcasts |
| **Authenticity** | Real emotions, real struggles, real consequences | Story investors, emotional fans |

## 5. HISTORICAL MODELS

| Event | Movement Type | Outcome |
|-------|---------------|---------|
| **ROH 2002 Formation** | Purist Revolution | Built from crash TV burnout and Attitude Era fatigue. Became workrate temple. |
| **AEW 2019 Formation** | Mixed (Rebellion + Restoration) | Indie revolution built from exile dissatisfaction with WWE corporate culture. Major success. |
| **FMW Late 80s Formation** | Outlaw Anarchy | Rejected traditional puroresu prestige model. Created deathmatch culture. |
| **ECW 1994-1995** | Outlaw Anarchy + Authenticity | Anti-corporate, ultra-violent, but also emotionally invested storytelling. Cult legend. |
| **NOAH 2000** | Purist Revolution (Restoration) | "True King's Road spirit" after AJPW political collapse. Massive initial success. |

### ROH Case Study

**Formation Cause:** Philosophical Vacuum. Post-Attitude Era, no major promotion offering pure workrate focus.

**Seed Composition:**
- Workers: Low Bray (limited star power initially)
- Charisma: Medium (indie darlings, not mainstream)
- Fan Loyalty: High (workrate purists hungry)
- Financial: Low (indie budget)
- Timing: Perfect (crash TV backlash peak)
- Philosophy: Crystal clear ("Code of Honor")

**Viability Score:** ~65 (sustainable with growth potential)

**Movement Type:** Purist Revolution

**Emotional Core:** Restoration ("Real wrestling still exists")

**Outcome:** Became talent development pipeline for major promotions. Cultural impact far exceeded financial success.

How movements are remembered based on success/failure:

| Outcome | Memory Type | Example |

## 8. PHILOSOPHY

> "New movements are born from pain. From exile. From the desperate belief that wrestling can be more than what the dying giants offer. Some movements change the world. Some burn bright and fade. All of them matter, because they prove that wrestling's soul cannot be owned by any single promotion."

New movements represent hope: that when the industry fails, passionate people will build something new. SCS must model both the romantic possibility and the harsh reality of movement-building.
