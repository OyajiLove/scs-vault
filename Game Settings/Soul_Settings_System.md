## Core Problem

SCS's emotional simulation can overwhelm players looking for a traditional "build your fed" sandbox game. Scars that linger, betrayals that poison for years, fanbases that resent choices, booking memory that doesn't forget mistakes.

This is what makes SCS art, not just simulation. But it's heavy.

**Solution:** Not to gut what makes SCS special. Offer mode toggles that let players engage on their terms, with dignity and clarity, not fake simplification.

## Philosophy

> "SCS can overwhelm players with the emotional weight it simulates. But that's not a problem, it's a sign of depth and seriousness. The solution is not to dumb it down. The solution is to offer clear soul-setting modes so players can choose: how much history they want to carry, how much consequence they can handle, how human they want this world to be."
