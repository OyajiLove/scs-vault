## Overview

Desperation Decisions occur when workers are forced to accept bookings, gimmicks, or contracts they would normally refuse because their options have dried up. This mechanic captures the tragic reality of wrestling careers: when financial pressure meets burned bridges meets industry change, even legends compromise their values.

**The Gordy Principle:** A worker who would never accept a cartoon gimmick at their peak will take anything when the money's gone, the bridges are burned, and the bills are due.

## Pressure Persistence Categories

Not all pressure decays the same way.

### Recoverable Pressures (decay with income)

| Factor | Decay Rate |
|--------|------------|
| Months without income | Resets to 0 when employed |
| Lifestyle maintenance gap | -1 per month with stable income |
| Debt (non-medical) | -1 per 6 months with income |
| Medical bills | -1 per year with income (very slow) |
| Family pressure | Reduces as bills get paid |

### Sticky Pressures (require specific conditions)

| Factor | Clears When |
|--------|-------------|
| Blacklisted from promotion | Regime change, 5+ years pass, or public rehabilitation |
| Reputation damage | Successful run elsewhere, 3+ years clean |
| Legal bar from country | Legal resolution |
| Industry-wide heat | Major promotion vouches for you |

### Permanent Pressures (never fully clear)

| Factor | Reality |
|--------|---------|
| Brain damage | Physical and cognitive ceiling lowered forever |
| Severe career-altering injury | May adapt, never the same |
| Age | Only goes up |
| Lost prime years | Gone forever |

## Pride Ceiling Mechanic

When a worker accepts a desperation gimmick, their Ego takes a hit and gains a **Pride Ceiling**: a cap on how high their Ego can recover.

### The Compromise Scar

| Compromise Severity | Ego Hit | Ceiling Drop |
|---------------------|---------|--------------|
| Slight repackage | -1 | -1 |
| Midcard demotion | -2 | -2 |
| Comedy gimmick | -3 | -3 |
| Masked/hooded (identity loss) | -4 | -4 |
| Jobber role | -4 | -5 |
| Humiliation angle | -5 | -6 |

**Gordy Example:**
- Pre-incident Ego: ~15
- Compromise (Executioner): -4 Ego, ceiling drops to 11
- Post-compromise Ego: 11
- Maximum recovery possible: 11 (not 15)

### Recovery Within Ceiling

Pride recovers slowly with positive events:

| Event | Ego Recovery |
|-------|--------------|
| Steady employment | +1 per year |
| Midcard success | +1 |
| Crowd responds positively | +1 |
| Peer respect (locker room) | +1 |
| Clean living (if substance history) | +1 |

Recovery stops at the ceiling.

### Ceiling Shatter Events

Rare. Requires extraordinary circumstances:

| Event | Effect |
|-------|--------|
| Unexpected world title run | Ceiling shatters, full recovery possible |
| Career-defining match (MOTY level) | Ceiling +3 |
| Industry-wide redemption narrative | Ceiling +2 |
| Rival promotion bidding war | Ceiling +2 |
| Hall of Fame level late run | Ceiling shatters |

### Multiple Compromises

Each compounds. Ceiling drops further. Recovery gets harder.

| Compromises | Cumulative Effect |
|-------------|-------------------|
| First | Ceiling - severity |
| Second | Ceiling - (severity Ã— 1.5) |
| Third+ | Ceiling - (severity Ã— 2) |

**Pride Floor:** Minimum Ego of 3. Below that, worker has no self-respect left, becomes purely transactional.

### Historical Examples

| Worker | Compromise | Recovery | Ceiling Shattered? |
|--------|------------|----------|-------------------|
| Terry Gordy | Executioner | None (died 2001) | No |
| Jake Roberts | Indies, shoots | DDP Yoga, HOF | Partially (legacy, not in-ring) |

## Narrative Applications

### Tragedy Arcs
- Watch beloved worker forced into humiliation
- Crowd memory of peak makes decline painful
- Can generate "save them" babyface sympathy if another worker intervenes

### Redemption Setups
- Desperate acceptance â†’ surprising success
- Worker finds new identity in forced role
- "They took my name, I made the hood legendary"
- Ceiling shatter moment becomes career-defining

### Cautionary Tales
- Young workers see veterans fall
- "Don't burn bridges, don't blow your money"
- Industry memory of falls from grace
- Can affect young worker personality development (seeing what happens)

### Player Moral Choices
- Exploit desperate workers (low cost, but morality implications)
- Offer dignity to fallen stars (higher cost, loyalty reward)
- How you treat desperate workers affects locker room perception

## Summary

The Desperation Decisions mechanic creates emergent tragedy and redemption narratives by modeling the real pressures that force workers into compromising positions. It respects that:

1. **Financial pressure is real** and compounds over time
2. **Options close** through blacklists, industry change, and physical decline
3. **Pride resists** but can be overwhelmed
4. **Compromise leaves scars** that limit recovery
5. **Some workers have safety nets** (family, fame, skills) and some don't
6. **Extraordinary success can rewrite the story** but it's rare

The Gordy Principle remains: when the money's gone and the bridges are burned, even legends put on the hood.
