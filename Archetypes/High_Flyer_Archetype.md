## Subtypes

### Vol 1 Foundation

| Subtype | Description | Examples |
|---------|-------------|----------|
| **Lucha Flyer** | Traditional Mexican high flying | Rey Mysterio, Psicosis, Juventud |
| **Dare Devil** | Risk taker, big bumps | Jeff Hardy, Sabu, Darby Allin |
| **Technical Flyer** | Aerial + mat work fusion | Ultimo Dragon, AJ Styles |
| **Power Flyer** | Surprising strength for size | PAC, Ricochet |
| **Spotmonkey** | All moves, no story (negative) | Various |
| **Storytelling Flyer** | Psychology-first aerial | Prime Rey, Hayabusa |

### Vol 2 Additions

| Subtype | Description | Examples |
|---------|-------------|----------|
| **Calculated Flyer** | Picks aerial spots smartly: calculated, beautiful execution. Not reckless. | Rey Mysterio (peak), Kota Ibushi (prime), Ultimo Dragon |
| **Gritty Underdog Flyer** | Uses aerials to survive: fighting from underneath, relying on speed and resilience. | Spike Dudley, Fuego Del Sol, early Rey Jr. |
| **Elite Cruiserweight** | Blends aerial excellence with technical polish and match structure discipline. | Eddie Guerrero (1997-1998), PAC (prime), Dragon Kid |
| **Unorthodox Acrobat** | Innovative, weird aerialists who create unpredictable offense. Strange angles, awkward timing, but brilliant spots. | Ricochet (early), El Phantasmo, Ninja Mack |
