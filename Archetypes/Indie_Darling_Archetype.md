## SUBTYPES

| Subtype | Core Concept | Examples |
|---------|--------------|----------|
| **Spot Monkey** | High spots over structure. Limited selling. Crowd pops over consistency. | Young Bucks (early), Jack Evans, Ricochet (pre-WWE) |
| **Flashy Technician** | Technical prowess with exaggerated, stylish execution. Moves look beautiful even when unnecessary. | Zack Sabre Jr. (early indie), Will Ospreay (transitional period) |
| **Cult Favourite** | Unique charisma or style earns hardcore following despite flaws. Not just about moves: aura, aesthetic, underground appeal. | Orange Cassidy, Nick Gage, Darby Allin |
| **Daredevil Scrapper** | Risk-taking brawler with indie ethos. Not pure flyer or technician: crash-and-burn fighter willing to bleed, break, or burn for a reaction. Flashy through madness, not polish. | Sami Callihan (early), Masada, Moxley (pre-WWE) |
| **Technical Virtuoso** | Smooth, intricate holds and counters with indie flair. Not flashy in high spots: flashy in complexity and chains. Wins over hardcore purists. | Jonathan Gresham, Timothy Thatcher (early), Kyle O'Reilly (ROH peak) |

## ASSOCIATED TAGS

| Tag | Meaning |
|-----|---------|
| **Flashy** | Prioritizes move beauty, spectacle, and visual pop. Risk-taking, even if psychology or selling suffers. Crowd loves the dazzle. |

### Flashy Tag Design Note

**Flashy is a TAG, not a skill.**

Rationale:
- Skill scores are about functional execution
- Tags are about flavor, audience perception, and meta
- Flashiness is often surface-level: you can be flashy but bad at match logic (early Jack Evans)
- Flexibility: some Indie Darlings evolve beyond being flashy

## BOOKING BEHAVIOR

- Expect showcase spots in multi-person matches
- May resist selling-heavy booking
- Perform best in spotfest environments
- Risk of crowd fatigue if overexposed without evolution
- Strong in tournament and showcase formats

---

## CAREER ARC

**Early Career:** Build cult following through spectacular style  
**Prime:** Peak athleticism, maximum spot output  
**Late Career:** Must evolve (add psychology, become Cult Favourite, or burn out)

---

## DESIGN NOTES

### Indie Darling vs. High Flyer

- High Flyer archetype focuses on aerial excellence as core identity
- Indie Darling is broader: flash and spectacle through any means
- Some Indie Darlings aren't primarily aerial (Technical Virtuoso, Daredevil Scrapper)

### Regional Reception

Indie Darling styles play differently based on audience:
- ROH/PWG audiences: Beloved
- Japanese audiences: Respected if technical foundation present
- Traditional territories: May be dismissed as "spotfest garbage"
- WWE developmental: Must add fundamentals to advance
