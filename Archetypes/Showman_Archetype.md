## SUBTYPES

### Main Event / Icon Tier

| Subtype | Core Concept | Examples |
|---------|--------------|----------|
| **Larger-Than-Life Icon** | Titans of charisma who transcend "good guy/bad guy" dynamics. They ARE the event. Pure star aura. | Hulk Hogan (face era), John Cena (peak), Tanahashi |
| **Mastermind Showman** | Over-the-top characters who weave emotion, psychology, and spectacle into every match and promo. Big talkers, bigger minds. | The Rock, Randy Savage, MJF (face-ish turns) |
| **Spectacle Warrior** | Wrestlers who own the arena through vivid imagery, overwhelming presence, and storytelling through body language. Matches can be short or strange, but moments feel like religious experiences. | Ultimate Warrior, Goldberg, Great Muta (mystique era), early Asuka |
| **Calculated Predator** | Cold, ruthless, psychology-driven Showmen who captivate through slow escalation, perfect timing, and sudden lethal strikes. Their charisma lies in lethal efficiency. (Vol 2 Chunk 03 addition) | Randy Orton (Apex Predator era), Triple H (Cerebral Assassin era), Jake Roberts (some phases), early Andrade |

### Performance Specialists

| Subtype | Core Concept | Examples |
|---------|--------------|----------|
| **Charismatic Daredevil** | Flashy high-flyers who win crowd love through physical spectacle. Risk takers for the pop. | The Rockers, young Jeff Hardy, early Kota Ibushi |
| **Emotional Performer** | Workers who draw deep crowd sympathy and emotional investment through vulnerability and passion, even without athletic excellence. | Dusty Rhodes, Sami Zayn (post-injury), Mick Foley (pathos side) |
| **Theatrical Trickster** | Showmen who combine antics, cunning, and playful villainy into crowd-pleasing or infuriating performances. | Eddie Guerrero (Lie Cheat Steal), Toru Yano, Johnny B. Badd |
| **Technical Showman** | Skilled wrestlers who integrate smooth workrate with crowd-popping showmanship. Underrated backbone of shows. | Chad Gable, Kurt Angle (comedic runs), Santino Marella (serious side) |

### Modern/NXT Tier

| Subtype | Core Concept | Examples |
|---------|--------------|----------|
| **Athletic Entertainer** | Green but physically gifted workers, often college athletes, fitness models, IG stars. Big upside but rough in-ring instincts early. | Oba Femi (hybrid), Tiffany Stratton (early NXT), Jade Cargill (early) |
| **Arrogant Athlete** | Sports world crossovers who see wrestling as beneath them. Smug but credible. | Early Baron Corbin, Happy Corbin era, Pat McAfee heel persona |
| **Reality Crossover Star** | Workers from reality TV, IG, Twitch. Brings crossover audience but often mocked by hardcore fans. | Logan Paul (early), The Miz (early MTV era), Mandy Rose (Tough Enough persona) |
| **Muscle Showstopper** | Big athletic bodies who mix bodybuilder presentation with major crowd pops. Muscle gods. | Lex Luger (Narcissist), Rick Rude, Bobby Lashley (early WWE) |

### Comedy/Gimmick Tier

| Subtype | Core Concept | Examples |
|---------|--------------|----------|
| **Lovable Dynamo** | Workers beloved for earnestness, energy, and comedic timing. Crowd favourites even without elite skills. | Otis, R-Truth, Santino Marella (silly side) |
| **Gimmick Attraction** | Pure gimmick workers: career midcarders or opening acts with colorful characters. Crowd either laughs with or at them. | Boogeyman, No Way Jose, Repo Man, Adam Rose, early Elias |
| **Occupational Gimmick Worker** | Gimmick workers given professions or cartoon personas to create easy hooks with kids and casual audiences. 90s New Generation staple. | Duke "The Dumpster" Droese, The Goon, IRS, Isaac Yankem DDS |
| **Broken Entertainer** | Workers who embrace absurd, surreal, "broken reality" character work to create unique mythology. Fourth-wall-breaking postmodern Showmen. | Broken Matt Hardy, Bray Wyatt (Firefly Funhouse), The Hurricane, Danhausen |

### Company Product Tier

| Subtype | Core Concept | Examples |
|---------|--------------|----------|
| **Pure Entertainer** | Workers trained primarily in WWE/Performance Center style. More about timing, entertainment beats, and character work than independent wrestling structure. "Company-made" products. | Baron Corbin (after polish), Charlotte Flair (early), Braun Strowman (early) |

## ASSOCIATED TAGS

### Core Showman Tags

| Tag | Meaning |
|-----|---------|
| **Pop Generator** | Bonus crowd pops on big moves/gestures, even in losses |
| **Momentum Surfer** | Builds quick momentum swings when crowd heat is high |
| **Emotion Sink** | Can absorb crowd energy: matches "feel" bigger when they're in the ring |
| **Audience Puppeteer** | Greater control over heel/face turns depending on storyline swings |

### Crossover/Marketing Tags

| Tag | Meaning |
|-----|---------|
| **Crossover Appeal** | Draws attention from non-traditional wrestling audiences |
| **Spotlight Seeker** | Boosts when main eventing or working with legends |
| **Model Appeal** | Boosts marketability and casual audience interest based on looks |

### Gimmick Tags

| Tag | Meaning |
|-----|---------|
| **Gimmick Loyalty** | Audience more willing to forgive bad matches if gimmick is strong |
| **Fast Pop Starter** | Immediate crowd reaction in openers and midcard matches |

## BOOKING BEHAVIOR

- Character work often more important than match quality
- Promo time is strength: maximize it
- Can carry weak opponents through character
- Shorter matches often acceptable (protect limitations)
- Risk of overexposure if not rotated
- Must balance entertainment with credibility

### Era Sensitivity

| Era | Importance |
|-----|------------|
| Territory Era | Important but secondary to in-ring |
| 1980s WWF | CRITICAL (Hogan template) |
| Attitude Era | Very high (Rock, Austin promos) |
| 2000s Ruthless Aggression | Sports entertainer dominance |
| Modern NXT/WWE | Essential for main event |

## DESIGN NOTES

### Showman vs. Charismatic Entertainer

Vol 1's Charismatic Entertainer was a 6-subtype foundation. Vol 2's Showman expands this to 16 subtypes covering:
- 80s cartoon characters
- 90s New Generation occupational gimmicks
- Attitude Era broken/wild characters
- 2000s Ruthless Aggression sports entertainer dominance
- Modern NXT/Performance Center athletic entertainer boom

### Model Appeal Tag

Workers with mainstream beauty/fitness model backgrounds receive automatic boosts in marketing/brand appeal, social media growth, and audience casual interest, even if their matches are bad. Doesn't guarantee "wrestling" success but brings clear advantages.

Examples: Stacy Keibler, Torrie Wilson, Mandy Rose, Early Trish Stratus
