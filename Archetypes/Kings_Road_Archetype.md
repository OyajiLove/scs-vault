## SUBTYPES

### Main Event Tier

| Subtype | Core Concept | Examples |
|---------|--------------|----------|
| **Orange Crush** | Idealistic, fiery young ace-in-training who absorbs brutal punishment but refuses to quit. The burning spirit kid earning love through struggle. | Young Kenta Kobashi (early 90s AJPW) |
| **Iron Man** | Grizzled, mythic ace whose body absorbs unspeakable punishment and delivers devastating, match-ending lariats and death moves. Triumph through sheer endurance. Forged spirit, living monument to survival. | Late-career Kenta Kobashi (NOAH champion era) |
| **Sumo King** | Heavier, sumo-influenced wrestler using mass, striking, and grit. Less finesse, more iron will and weight-based offense. | Akira Taue, Early Genichiro Tenryu |

|---------|---------------------|
| **Structured Escalation Engine** | Matches have phases: base phase ‚Üí heat phase ‚Üí escalation phase ‚Üí killshot phase |

## KING'S ROAD VS. FIGHTING SPIRIT

| Aspect | King's Road (AJPW/NOAH) | Fighting Spirit (NJPW) |
|--------|-------------------------|------------------------|
| Pace | Slower build, gradual escalation | Higher tempo, aggressive, urgent |
| Offense vs Defense | Defense-focused (resist, survive, counter) | Offense-focused (attack, dominate, overwhelm) |
| Match Flow | Predictable structure: slow base ‚Üí injury tease ‚Üí big escalation | Less predictable, finishes can happen at any point |
| Move Philosophy | Big escalation, head drops, exhaustion finishes | Submissions and strikes credible at all times |
| Emotion | Spirit shown through enduring increasing punishment step-by-step | Spirit shown in sudden survival, desperate comebacks |
| Injury Risk | Extremely high in final escalation stages | Moderate, tied to submission damage or KO |
| Psychology | Classical storytelling, mythmaking, mortality in-ring | Martial arts/military realism emphasis |

**Summary:** King's Road = Tragedies. Fighting Spirit = Fights.

## HISTORICAL CONTEXT

### Head Drop Escalation (1990s)

The head drop phenomenon evolved naturally through the decade:
- Late 80s/Early 90s: Relatively minimal
- Mid 90s: Began escalating as workers survived more moves
- Late 90s: Natural consequence of showing guts and building on past defeats
- Workers pushed boundaries to overcome previous losses
- Misawa's death (2009) represented the tragic endpoint of this philosophy

### Choshu Influence

When Riki Choshu came to AJPW, he updated the style with:
- Increased aggression
- Higher tempo
- This created the bridge between older NWA-influenced King's Road and the explosive 90s version

## BOOKING BEHAVIOR

- Expect long matches with gradual escalation
- Title matches should be wars, not sprints
- Losses can build stars when handled heroically
- Near-falls create drama and investment
- Head drops and dangerous moves should be rare but impactful
- Series matches benefit from escalating stakes

---

## CAREER ARC

**Early Career:** Young lion taking punishment, proving heart (Orange Crush)  
**Rising:** Building credibility through losses, earning fan investment  
**Prime:** Escalation wars, title reigns, carrying the style  
**Late Career:** Transitioning to mentor role, or Hard-Luck Hero arc, or broken legend

## DESIGN NOTES

### King's Road vs. Other Puroresu Styles

- **King's Road:** AJPW/NOAH structured tragedy
- **Fighting Spirit:** NJPW aggressive martial arts realism
- **Muga:** Technical purity, ego-death wrestling
- **Junior Puroresu:** Speed and spectacle within puroresu framework

### Injury System Integration

King's Road matches should have:
- Higher baseline injury risk in escalation phases
- Head drop moves flagged as high-risk
- Cumulative damage tracking across match
- Career-threatening injury potential in main events
