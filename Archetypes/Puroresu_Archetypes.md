## Stoic Ace (#264)

**Core Skills:** Believability (high), Overness (high), Psychology (very high), Durability (high), Brawling or Technical (one must be solid), Selling (high), Stamina (good to elite), Safety (solid)

**Subtypes:**
| Subtype | Description | Examples |
|---------|-------------|----------|
| Warrior Saint | Almost mythological aura, endless heart | Mitsuharu Misawa |
| Stern Frontliner | Grittier, grimier | Takada after UWF |

**Tags:** Iron Will (exceptional resistance to cumulative fatigue across tournaments/title runs, but if pushed too far = catastrophic injuries)

**Character Notes:** Don't brag, don't chase headlines. Show up, fight legends, win wars, leave broken but standing. Cool-headed, composed, focused. Not attention-seeking. Dignity is brand.

**Fan Reaction:** Drawn to them for perseverance not flash. Massive loyalty and respect over time.

**CRITICAL NOTE:** Kawada + Hokuto are NOT Stoic Aces. They are Defiant Soul.

## Defiant Soul (#265)

**Core Skills:** Selling (high to elite), Believability (high), Psychology (very high), Stamina (high)

**Other Valued:** Brawling or Technical (varies), Durability (good but not unbreakable, part of pathos is eventual breakdown)

**Subtypes:**
| Subtype | Description | Examples |
|---------|-------------|----------|
| Iron Rebel | More outwardly aggressive | Kawada breaking Misawa's orbital |
| Tragic Survivor | Endlessly resilient but fragile | Hokuto carrying catastrophic injuries |

**Tags:** Beauty In Struggle (boosts emotional investment in major matches, especially long arcs of hardship)

**Philosophy:**
> "I see the ceiling above me. I know it's real. I know I'll smash myself against it and fail. I know it will hurt. I will fight anyway. Because even if I lose, my struggle itself is proof that I lived."

**Fan Reaction:** Intense sympathy, emotional investment, bittersweet attachment. Fighting fate itself.

**Character Notes:** Never the "chosen one." Scratch, claw, bleed, maybe touch greatness once or twice.

**CRITICAL NOTE:** NOT an Ace archetype. They're the antithesis of Ace.

**Examples:** Toshiaki Kawada, Akira Hokuto, Genichiro Tenryu (early), Hiroshi Hase (elements)

## Muga Practitioner (#268)

**Definition:** "Muga" (ç„¡æˆ‘) = "selflessness" or "ego death" - clean, pure, technical wrestling

**Core Skills:** Technical (very high), Psychology (very high), Basics (high), Believability (strong), Endurance (good to excellent)

**Other Valued:** Selling (solid, understated), Stamina (strong for grind matches), Charisma (medium, not bombastic, resonate via dignity)

**Subtypes:**
| Subtype | Description | Examples |
|---------|-------------|----------|
| Pure Muga/Stoic Technician | Classic muga master, calm, methodical chain wrestling | Fujinami, Nishimura |
| Dragon Showman | Strong fundamentals with crowd-pleasing showmanship | Young Tanahashi, Mutoh |
| Cold Artisan | Elegant, graceful but emotionally detached | Sanada |

**Tags:**
- Mat Master (bonus in matches based on sustained matwork, chain wrestling, transition logic)
- Respectful Craftsman (more likely to get over via "respect matches")

**Key Design Notes:**
- Excel at making long matches feel natural without "epic" melodrama
- Struggle if promotions/fans demand bomb-throwing flash
- Best as backbones for title divisions, technical showcases, stabilizers for chaotic scenes

**Personality:** Reserved, serious, purist. No wasted movement, no unnecessary flash.

**Fan Reaction:** Admired by hardcore wrestling purists. Sometimes "boring" to casuals unless paired with emotional workers.

**Examples:** Tatsumi Fujinami (originator), Osamu Nishimura, younger Hiroshi Tanahashi, early Mutoh, Hideki Suzuki, Sanada
