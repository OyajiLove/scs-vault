## BOOKING BEHAVIOR

- Accept losses gracefully (high loss tolerance before morale impact)
- Don't demand main event spots
- Reliable for TV matches, house shows, opening/midcard matches
- Excellent for putting over rising talent
- Low political backstage presence

---

## CAREER ARC

**Early Career:** Development as reliable midcarder  
**Prime:** Backbone of roster, making others look great  
**Late Career:** May transition to road agent, trainer, or occasional "thank you" veteran push  

---

## DESIGN NOTES

### Good Hand vs. Jobber

- Good Hands are SKILLED workers who lose often
- Jobbers are ENHANCEMENT workers who exist primarily to lose
- Good Hands can win occasionally and remain credible
- Good Hands maintain professional respect; Jobbers may be treated as expendable

### Real-World Context

The Good Hand is wrestling's invisible architect: trusted by bookers, adored by peers, respected by the sharpest fans. The spotlight may miss them, but greatness never does.
