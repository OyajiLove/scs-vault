## 1. ARTIFACT TYPES (5 CATEGORIES)

| Artifact | Emotional Weight | Persistence |
|----------|------------------|-------------|
| **Championship Belts** | Belts symbolize eras, heroes, betrayals. Carry emotional aura independent of current holders. | VERY HIGH |
| **Arenas and Territories** | Certain arenas (Korakuen Hall, Omni Atlanta, Budokan) emotionally anchor wrestling eras and fan loyalty | HIGH |
| **Promotion Logos and Branding** | Logos, colors, slogans trigger emotional memory instantly, decades after promotion death | HIGH |
| **Music and Themes** | Entrance themes, opening signature music emotionally anchor fans to eras | MEDIUM-HIGH |
| **Photographs, Magazines, Posters** | Iconic images preserve emotional myths permanently | MEDIUM |

---

## 2. ARTIFACT BEHAVIORS (4 MECHANICS)

| Behavior | Effect |
|----------|--------|
| **Memory Anchoring** | Promotions/events using original belts, logos, arenas trigger emotional nostalgia boosts automatically for fans attached to old eras |
| **Artifact Betrayal Scars** | Changing belt designs, retiring arenas, rebranding logos without emotional care can cause loyalty decay |
| **Mythological Artifact Survival** | Long-dead promotions' belts/logos may survive in indie promotions, rebel movements, nostalgic revivals, carrying emotional legitimacy across generations |
| **Fake Revivals Risk** | Corporate reuses of old artifacts (without emotional authenticity) may trigger resentment if seen as cynical exploitation |

## 3. CHAMPIONSHIP BELTS (Deep Dive)

### Belt Emotional Tiers

| Tier | Description | Example |
|------|-------------|---------|
| **Legendary** | Transcends promotion, carries independent emotional weight | Big Gold Belt (NWA/WCW/WWE) |
| **Iconic** | Defines era or promotion identity | WWF Winged Eagle, IWGP Heavyweight |
| **Significant** | Important within context, strong fan attachment | NWA World Tag Team, AJPW Triple Crown |
| **Standard** | Functional title belt, moderate attachment | Most secondary titles |
| **Forgettable** | Poorly designed or short-lived, minimal attachment | Various short-term belts |

### Big Gold Belt Case Study

**Origin:** NWA World Heavyweight Championship (1986)

**Emotional Journey:**
- NWA era: Symbol of serious world champion lineage, traveling champion tradition
- WCW era: Carried NWA legitimacy into Turner era, icon of Monday Night Wars
- WWE acquisition: Emotional tension, "wrong" feeling for traditionalists
- Current: Historical artifact, occasionally resurfaces for nostalgia

**Why It Mattered:**
- Physical design communicated "serious champion" instantly
- Lineage connected holders to wrestling history
- Survived multiple promotion deaths
- Remains emotionally resonant decades later

### Belt Change Betrayal Examples

| Change | Fan Reaction |
|--------|--------------|
| **WWE "Spinner" Belt** | Initial mockery, eventual acceptance, nostalgia now |
| **Universal Championship (Red)** | Criticized as toy-like, brand-split confusion |
| **IWGP World Heavyweight** | Combining lineages controversial for purists |
| **AEW World Title Design** | Generally accepted, clean aesthetic |

## 4. ARENAS AND TERRITORIES

### Sacred Venues

| Venue | Emotional Weight | Why |
|-------|------------------|-----|
| **Budokan Hall** | LEGENDARY | "Holy ground" for puroresu. Hosting show there triggers generational loyalty memories. |
| **Korakuen Hall** | ICONIC | Intimate venue, hardcore fan atmosphere, countless legendary matches |
| **Madison Square Garden** | LEGENDARY | "World's most famous arena," legitimacy anchor for any promotion |
| **Tokyo Dome** | ICONIC | NJPW flagship, Wrestle Kingdom, mainstream legitimacy |
| **ECW Arena** | SIGNIFICANT | Cult status, hardcore movement birthplace |

### Venue Change Effects

| Action | Effect |
|--------|--------|
| **Running sacred venue** | +15-30 prestige boost, nostalgia trigger |
| **Abandoning traditional venue** | -10-25 loyalty with traditionalists |
| **Returning to venue after absence** | Homecoming boost, emotional storyline potential |
| **Venue closure** | Mourning period, memory crystallization |

## 5. LOGOS AND BRANDING

### Logo Emotional Anchoring

| Element | Effect |
|---------|--------|
| **Original Logo** | Instant era recognition, nostalgia trigger |
| **Slight Modernization** | Generally accepted if care shown (NJPW Lion Mark) |
| **Major Redesign** | Risk of alienating traditionalists |
| **Retro Revival** | Nostalgia marketing, fan service |
| **Corporate Sanitization** | Often criticized as "soulless" |

| Logo | Status |
|------|--------|
| **NJPW Lion Mark** | Powerful emotional brand. Slight modernizations accepted because care maintained legacy. |
| **WWF Scratch Logo** | Defined Attitude Era. Legal change to WWE felt like identity loss. |

## 9. PHILOSOPHY

> "Objects remember. A belt that touched Flair's waist carries that touch forever, even on a replica, even in a museum case. An arena where legends bled holds that blood in its concrete. A logo that meant rebellion still means rebellion, even after the company dies. These aren't just things. They're emotional anchors that connect generations to moments they never lived but somehow still feel."

Artifacts create continuity across wrestling's fragmented history. They're how fans connect to eras they never saw, and how promotions claim legitimacy they haven't earned.
