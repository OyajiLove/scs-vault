## 1. CAUSES FOR FAN PRESERVATION (4 TRIGGERS)

| Cause | Description | Fan Response |
|-------|-------------|--------------|
| **Promotional Neglect** | Promotions abandon fallen workers, eras, or artifacts due to scandal fear, leadership change, or commercial priorities | Fans preserve what corporations forget |
| **Corporate Whitewashing** | Promotions sanitize history, erasing uncomfortable or non-marketable truths | Fans preserve authentic memory |
| **Cultural Shifts** | Official narratives drift, but hardcore fanbases refuse to let emotional truths be erased | Fans resist revisionism |
| **Outlaw Spirit** | Wrestling's anarchist roots foster fan-driven projects independent of official corporate histories | Fans assert independence |

## 4. HISTORICAL MODELS

| Fan Action | Outcome |
|------------|---------|
| **DVDVR Yearbooks** | Saved emotional memory of early 90s WCW TV greatness from total erasure. Introduced generations of fans to forgotten classics. Anchored workers like Brian Pillman's early WCW work permanently. |
| **Indie Tournaments Honoring Misawa** | Small promotions keeping King's Road emotionally alive after NOAH and AJPW drifted. Style preservation through action. |
| **Independent Eddie Guerrero Tributes** | Local shows remembering Guerrero authentically while WWE increasingly commercialized memory. Counter-narrative to corporate handling. |
| **Joshi Tape Trading Networks** | 1990s fan networks preserving AJW/JWP content that would otherwise be lost. Created foundation for later rediscovery boom. |

### DVDVR Yearbooks Case Study

**Project:** Death Valley Driver Video Review annual yearbook project

**Content:** Comprehensive reviews of matches across all promotions, preserving critical consensus on forgotten work

**Impact:**
- Early 90s WCW TV matches preserved in critical memory
- Workers like Pillman, Arn Anderson given "workrate legitimacy" outside mainstream narrative
- Created reference framework for future historians
- Influenced what later documentarians considered worth covering

**Tipping Point:** Reached cult status in late 90s, permanent anchor by mid-2000s

**Legacy:** DVDVR approach influenced every serious wrestling community that followed. "DVDVR-style" became shorthand for hardcore critical preservation.

## 5. FAN VS CORPORATE NARRATIVE BATTLES

When fan preservation contradicts official history:

| Scenario | Fan Narrative | Corporate Narrative | Resolution |
|----------|---------------|---------------------|------------|
| **Worker Erased** | "They were great, history is wrong" | Silence or dismissal | Fan narrative persists in underground |
| **Era Whitewashed** | "Here's what really happened" | Sanitized version promoted | Competing memories coexist |
| **Style Abandoned** | "This philosophy matters" | "Times have changed" | Fan tournaments preserve style |
| **Tragedy Exploited** | "This dishonors the memory" | Corporate continues | Fan cynicism, counter-projects |

### Cultural Battle Resolution

| Outcome | Conditions |
|---------|------------|
| **Fan Narrative Wins** | Corporate collapses, leadership changes, cultural shift favors authenticity |
| **Corporate Narrative Wins** | Fan community shrinks, mainstream absorption, time passage |
| **Permanent Coexistence** | Both persist in different spaces, neither dominant |
| **Synthesis** | Corporate eventually adopts fan framing (rare but possible) |

## 9. PHILOSOPHY

> "When corporations forget, fans remember. When official history lies, fan history tells the truth. DVDVR yearbooks saved early 90s WCW TV from erasure. Tape trading networks preserved joshi wrestling for a generation that would rediscover it. Indie tribute shows keep King's Road alive when AJPW and NOAH drift. The outlaw spirit of wrestling lives in fans who refuse to let the flame die, even when no one's paying them to keep it burning."

Fan-led preservation is wrestling's immune system against corporate memory erasure. It ensures the important things survive.
