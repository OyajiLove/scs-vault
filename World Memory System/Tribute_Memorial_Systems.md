## 1. TRIBUTE ACT TYPES (5 CATEGORIES)

| Act Type | Description | Emotional Impact |
|----------|-------------|------------------|
| **Memorial Shows** | Full dedicated events honoring a fallen worker or legendary figure. Ticket revenue often donated to families or foundations. | VERY HIGH |
| **Tribute Matches** | Special matches at regular shows where workers dedicate performances to fallen figures (e.g., Bret/Benoit for Owen) | HIGH |
| **Public Donations** | Prominent workers donate proceeds, bonuses, or personal money to survivors, GoFundMe-type relief campaigns (especially for indie workers) | MEDIUM-HIGH |
| **Emotional Interviews/Segments** | Shoots, promos, media appearances where workers or bookers publicly honor fallen comrades | MEDIUM |
| **Scholarships and Awards** | Annual awards/tournaments created in name of fallen figures to preserve legacy over decades (e.g., Owen Hart Foundation Tournament) | VERY HIGH (long-term) |

## 2. EMOTIONAL EFFECTS (4 OUTCOMES)

| Effect | Behavior | Duration |
|--------|----------|----------|
| **Fan Loyalty Surges** | Authentic tributes cause massive emotional loyalty boosts toward promotions and workers involved | 6 months - 2 years |
| **World Memory Anchoring** | Tribute events solidify the fallen worker's emotional legacy in future world memory permanently | Permanent |
| **Worker Morale Boosts** | Locker rooms emotionally unify around sincere tributes. Morale, trust, and loyalty stats spike temporarily. | 1-6 months |
| **Scandal Risk if Mishandled** | If tributes are insincere, commercialized, or politicized, fans backlash harshly | Immediate + long-term reputation damage |

### Effect Magnitude by Tribute Type

| Tribute Type | Fan Loyalty | Memory Anchor | Morale Boost | Scandal Risk |
|--------------|-------------|---------------|--------------|--------------|
| **Memorial Show** | +25-40% | Permanent | +30 | HIGH if mishandled |
| **Tribute Match** | +15-25% | Strong | +20 | MEDIUM |
| **Public Donation** | +10-20% | Moderate | +25 | LOW |
| **Emotional Segment** | +10-15% | Moderate | +15 | MEDIUM |
| **Scholarship/Award** | +5-10% (ongoing) | Very Strong | +10 | LOW |

## 3. SIMULATION MECHANICS

| Mechanic | Behavior |
|----------|----------|
| **Automatic Tribute Generation** | When major workers or figures die unexpectedly, promotions/workers with emotional loyalty may organize tribute matches or shows dynamically |
| **Player-Driven Tributes** | Player-controlled promotions/workers can organize tributes. Sincere = emotional loyalty and brand trust increase. Exploitative = backlash. |
| **Charity Mechanic** | Revenue from tribute shows can optionally be redirected to in-world "survivor funds," boosting emotional resonance permanently |
| **Worker Voluntary Donations** | High loyalty, morality, or traditionalism workers more likely to donate spontaneously or push for memorial events without being booked |

### Automatic Tribute Triggers

| Trigger | Probability | Response Type |
|---------|-------------|---------------|
| **Legendary Worker Death** | 95% | Major memorial show within 1 month |
| **Icon Death** | 80% | Tribute show or matches within 2 months |
| **Established Star Death** | 60% | Tribute matches at next major show |
| **Indie/Lower Card Death** | 30% | Individual worker donations, small tributes |
| **Tragic Accident (any tier)** | +25% to base | Accelerated response, cross-promotional support |

## 4. HISTORICAL MODELS

| Tribute Event | Emotional Impact |
|---------------|------------------|
| **Pillman Memorial Shows (1998-2001)** | Cross-promotional support, massive emotional outpouring, grassroots rebuilding of indie solidarity networks. Showed wrestling could transcend promotion wars. |
| **Bret/Benoit Owen Hart Tribute Match (WCW, 1999)** | Serious emotional catharsis match. Fans emotionally invested in sincerity, despite promotion boundaries. Proved grief transcends kayfabe. |
| **AEW Owen Hart Foundation Tournament** | Respectful, family-approved tribute building generational loyalty resonance without exploitative vibes. Model for long-term legacy preservation. |
| **Jericho/Other Donations (Real-Life GoFundMe)** | Strengthened perception of wrestling family solidarity even among wrestlers across promotions. Modern indie support network. |

### Owen Hart Foundation Case Study

**Background:** Owen Hart died in WWF ring accident (1999). Martha Hart refused WWE involvement in tributes for decades.

**AEW Approach (2022+):**
- Family approval secured first
- Foundation partnership, not just branding
- Tournament format creates ongoing legacy
- Scholarship component ensures real-world impact
- Presentation respectful, not exploitative

**Result:**
- Massive fan approval
- Martha Hart's public endorsement
- Annual tradition established
- Owen's legacy preserved for new generation
- Model for sincere corporate tribute

**Key Lesson:** Family approval + ongoing commitment + real charity = successful long-term tribute.

## 5. MISHANDLED TRIBUTE WARNING SIGNS

| Warning Sign | Risk Level |
|--------------|------------|
| **Family Opposition** | CRITICAL (do not proceed) |
| **Immediate Merchandise Push** | HIGH |
| **Using Death for Storyline Heat** | VERY HIGH |
| **Exploiting Tragedy for Ratings** | VERY HIGH |
| **No Charity/Foundation Component** | MEDIUM |
| **Rushed, Poorly Planned Event** | MEDIUM |
| **Political Backstage Drama Visible** | HIGH |

### Exploitation Detection

| Factor | Sincere Indicator | Exploitative Indicator |
|--------|-------------------|------------------------|
| **Family Involvement** | Consulted and supportive | Excluded or opposed |
| **Charity Component** | Significant proceeds donated | Token or none |
| **Presentation Tone** | Somber, respectful | Sensationalized |
| **Timing** | Appropriate mourning period | Rushed for ratings |
| **Worker Involvement** | Genuine grief visible | Forced participation |
| **Follow-Through** | Ongoing legacy efforts | One-time event |

## 6. SCANDAL EXAMPLES (WHAT NOT TO DO)

| Example | What Went Wrong | Consequence |
|---------|-----------------|-------------|
| **Eddie Guerrero Exploitation (WWE 2005-2006)** | Used Eddie's death repeatedly for storyline heat, "Eddie's in hell" angle, exploitative merchandising | Major fan backlash, damaged WWE credibility with Latino audience |
| **Benoit Memory Erasure** | Complete removal vs acknowledgment of complicated legacy | Ongoing fan debate, no closure |
| **Rushed Tribute Shows** | Events planned before proper mourning | Feels performative, not sincere |

### Eddie Guerrero Case Study

**Initial Tribute:** Sincere memorial show, emotional promos, genuine grief. Positive response.

**Exploitation Phase:**
- "I'm doing this for Eddie" became storyline crutch
- Rey Mysterio pushed using Eddie's memory repeatedly
- "Eddie's in hell" Randy Orton promo
- Excessive merchandise

**Fan Response:**
- Initial goodwill eroded
- Latino fans particularly offended
- Long-term trust damage
- Became canonical example of exploitation

**Lesson:** Initial tribute can be sincere. Problem is prolonged exploitation. There's a line between honoring and using.

## 9. WORKER DONATION SYSTEM

High-profile workers donating to lower-card or indie workers:

| Donation Type | Effect |
|---------------|--------|
| **Public GoFundMe Contribution** | +10-20 solidarity perception, +5 donor popularity |
| **Private Donation (Later Revealed)** | +15-25 solidarity perception, +10 donor popularity |
| **Organizing Relief Fund** | +20-30 solidarity perception, +15 donor popularity, leadership recognition |
| **Ongoing Support** | +25-35 solidarity perception, permanent legacy association |

### Donation Triggers

| Situation | Likely Donors |
|-----------|---------------|
| **Career-Ending Injury** | Close friends, same promotion, high morality workers |
| **Medical Emergency** | Locker room leaders, financially secure veterans |
| **Family Tragedy** | Personal connections, high empathy workers |
| **Indie Worker Struggle** | Veterans who remember struggling, high traditionalism |

## 10. PHILOSOPHY

> "Workers don't just build legacy through championships: they build it through how they're remembered and how they remember others. Promotions show their real soul not in booking alone, but in how they honor the fallen. This is where wrestling's emotional bloodstream comes alive: loss, loyalty, gratitude, grief, pride, guilt, forgiveness."

Tribute systems reveal character. They show whether wrestling is a family or just a business. The difference between exploitation and honor defines a promotion's soul.
