## 1. WHEN FANBASES BEGIN EMOTIONAL EXODUS

| Trigger | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Golden Era Collapse** | Emotional investment no longer rewarded: betrayal, stagnation, scandals |
| **Worker Exodus** | Icons, beloved midcarders, emotional anchors leave or are buried: fans follow their heroes |
| **Booking Cultural Drift** | Promotion philosophy mutates (e.g., from serious prestige to irony or crash TV): purist fans abandon ship |
| **Scandal Fallout** | Fans reject promotion on moral grounds: racism, abuse, systemic corruption exposed |
| **External Magnetic Alternatives** | New promotions or movements capture displaced emotional needs |

### Trigger Thresholds

| Trigger | Threshold |
|---------|-----------|
| **Golden Era Collapse** | Stage 3+ collapse (Mass Migration stage) |
| **Worker Exodus** | 3+ beloved workers leave within 12 months |
| **Booking Cultural Drift** | Philosophy score shifts 40+ points from fanbase preferences |
| **Scandal Fallout** | Severity 7+ scandal with inadequate response |
| **External Alternatives** | Rival promotion emotional score exceeds current by 30+ points |

## 2. FAN EXODUS BEHAVIORS (4 TYPES)

| Behavior | Effect |
|----------|--------|
| **Silent Drifting** | Fans quietly stop attending, stop buying merch, reduce TV engagement: without rage |
| **Public Defection** | Loud fans publicly denounce promotion and align with rivals |
| **Cult-Loyalist Retrenchment** | Hardcore loyalists stay behind: often toxic fanbases form around decaying brand identity |
| **Memory Nostalgia Cults** | Fans emotionally detach but preserve loyalty to Golden Era memories: not the current product |

### Behavior Distribution by Trigger

| Trigger | Silent | Public | Retrenchment | Nostalgia |
|---------|--------|--------|--------------|-----------|
| **Golden Era Collapse** | 40% | 20% | 15% | 25% |
| **Worker Exodus** | 30% | 35% | 10% | 25% |
| **Cultural Drift** | 50% | 25% | 5% | 20% |
| **Scandal Fallout** | 25% | 50% | 10% | 15% |
| **External Alternatives** | 45% | 30% | 5% | 20% |

## 3. HISTORICAL EXAMPLES

| Event | Outcome |
|-------|---------|

## 4. SIMULATION MECHANICS

| Mechanic | Behavior |
|----------|----------|
| **Emotional Loyalty Threshold** | Fans with high emotional investment feel betrayal harder: exodus velocity increases after collapse |
| **Demographic Shifts** | Promotions that lose core emotional fans may replace them with new demographics: fragmenting or reshaping brand identity |
| **World Memory Tags** | Promotions tagged historically: "Lost Purist Fanbase (YYYY)", "Cultist Retrenchment Era", etc. |
| **New Promotion Loyalty Births** | Exiled fans strengthen rival promotions' emotional cores rapidly if new homes reward emotional investment properly |

### Exodus Velocity Calculation

| Factor | Effect on Velocity |
|--------|-------------------|
| **High prior investment** | +50% velocity (betrayal cuts deeper) |
| **Low prior investment** | -30% velocity (less emotional stake) |
| **Strong external alternative** | +40% velocity |
| **No clear alternative** | -50% velocity (nowhere to go) |
| **Multiple simultaneous triggers** | +25% per additional trigger |

### World Memory Tags (Examples)

| Tag | Meaning |
|-----|---------|
| **Lost Purist Fanbase (YYYY)** | Core traditional fans departed en masse |
| **Cultist Retrenchment Era** | Remaining fans became insular and toxic |
| **Demographic Replacement (YYYY)** | Original fanbase replaced by different demographic |
| **Exodus Destination: [Promotion]** | Where fans migrated to |

## 5. DEMOGRAPHIC SHIFT MECHANICS

When core fans leave, promotions may replace them:

| Scenario | Outcome |
|----------|---------|
| **Replace with similar demographic** | Brand identity preserved, slower recovery |
| **Replace with different demographic** | Brand identity mutates, faster recovery but different product |
| **Fail to replace** | Attendance/ratings decline, financial pressure |
| **Partial replacement** | Fragmented fanbase, identity crisis |

### Replacement Demographics

| Original Fanbase | Potential Replacements |
|------------------|----------------------|
| **Hardcore purists** | Casual entertainment fans |
| **Attitude Era adults** | Family-friendly PG audience |
| **Territorial traditionalists** | National TV casual viewers |
| **Workrate enthusiasts** | Spectacle/character fans |
