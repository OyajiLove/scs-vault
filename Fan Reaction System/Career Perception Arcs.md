## Career Stages

Workers move through distinct career stages, each with unique fan perception patterns:

| Stage | Description | Typical Duration |
|-------|-------------|------------------|
| **Rookie** | New to major promotion; unproven | 1-3 years |
| **Breakout** | First major push or viral moment | 6 months - 2 years |
| **Established Star** | Proven draw with stable position | 3-10 years |
| **Peak** | Career zenith; maximum influence | 1-5 years |
| **Decline** | Physical/creative erosion begins | 2-5 years |
| **Crisis** | Scandal, injury, or career threat | Variable |
| **Reinvention** | Attempted persona/style change | 1-3 years |
| **Legacy** | Post-peak; trading on past glory | Indefinite |

## Stage-Specific Fan Perception

### Rookie Stage

| Perception Element | Fan Tendency |
|--------------------|--------------|
| Benefit of Doubt | High (still proving themselves) |
| Scandal Tolerance | Medium (mistakes expected, not excused) |
| Expectations | Low (room to grow) |
| Turn Risk | Low (not invested enough to turn) |
| Narrative Frame | "Future star" or "green talent" |

**Fan Behaviors:**
- Curiosity about potential
- Forgiveness for rough edges
- Excitement when they show flashes
- Quick to dismiss if boring

---

### Breakout Stage

| Perception Element | Fan Tendency |
|--------------------|--------------|
| Benefit of Doubt | Very High (honeymoon period) |
| Scandal Tolerance | High (protected by momentum) |
| Expectations | Rising rapidly |
| Turn Risk | Low-Medium (bandwagon building) |
| Narrative Frame | "The next big thing" |

**Fan Behaviors:**
- Surge of support and investment
- "Discovered them first" pride (especially indie/smark fans)
- Bandwagon fans join
- Protective of "their" rising star
- Hypersensitive to bad booking

---

### Established Star Stage

| Perception Element | Fan Tendency |
|--------------------|--------------|
| Benefit of Doubt | Medium (track record matters now) |
| Scandal Tolerance | Medium-High (loyalty built up) |
| Expectations | High and stable |
| Turn Risk | Medium (complacency can breed resentment) |
| Narrative Frame | "Reliable draw" or "company pillar" |

**Fan Behaviors:**
- Stable loyalty, less volatility
- Higher tolerance for missteps
- Brand identification with worker
- May take them for granted
- React strongly to perceived burial

---

### Peak Stage

| Perception Element | Fan Tendency |
|--------------------|--------------|
| Benefit of Doubt | High (earned through excellence) |
| Scandal Tolerance | Highest (at maximum protection) |
| Expectations | Maximum |
| Turn Risk | Medium (backlash if they "change") |
| Narrative Frame | "The best in the world" |

**Fan Behaviors:**
- Maximum investment and loyalty
- Will defend against critics
- Expect greatness every appearance
- Sensitive to any sign of decline
- "They've earned the right" tolerance

---

### Decline Stage

| Perception Element | Fan Tendency |
|--------------------|--------------|
| Benefit of Doubt | Eroding |
| Scandal Tolerance | Medium (goodwill depleting) |
| Expectations | Complicated (nostalgia vs reality) |
| Turn Risk | High (if they "won't move on") |
| Narrative Frame | "Past their prime" or "still got it" |

**Fan Behaviors:**
- Nostalgia vs "move on" tension
- Sensitivity to "burying young talent"
- Appreciation for graceful decline
- Resentment if clinging to spotlight
- "One more great match" hope

---

### Crisis Stage

| Perception Element | Fan Tendency |
|--------------------|--------------|
| Benefit of Doubt | Variable (depends on crisis type) |
| Scandal Tolerance | Being tested directly |
| Expectations | Suspended (waiting to see) |
| Turn Risk | Very High |
| Narrative Frame | "Fallen star" or "victim of circumstance" |

**Fan Behaviors:**
- Fanbase type determines response (see [[Fanbase Types]])
- Loyalty tested to breaking point
- Some defend, some attack, some wait
- Media narrative heavily influences casuals
- Defines long-term legacy perception

---

### Reinvention Stage

| Perception Element | Fan Tendency |
|--------------------|--------------|
| Benefit of Doubt | Medium (skepticism but hope) |
| Scandal Tolerance | Medium |
| Expectations | Cautious |
| Turn Risk | Medium (if reinvention feels fake) |
| Narrative Frame | "Redemption arc" or "desperate rebrand" |

**Fan Behaviors:**
- Skepticism vs hope tension
- Authenticity heavily scrutinized
- Genuine evolution celebrated
- Desperate clinging mocked
- "Nostalgia pop" for old persona

---

### Legacy Stage

| Perception Element | Fan Tendency |
|--------------------|--------------|
| Benefit of Doubt | High (earned lifetime pass) |
| Scandal Tolerance | High (but not unlimited) |
| Expectations | Adjusted (special attraction, not workhorse) |
| Turn Risk | Low (unless major scandal) |
| Narrative Frame | "Living legend" or "part-timer clogging spots" |

**Fan Behaviors:**
- Protective nostalgia
- "Earned the right" tolerance
- Special attraction booking accepted
- Resentment if blocking young talent
- Legacy matches carry extra weight

## Success Changes People (Fan Awareness)

Fans notice and react when success changes workers' behavior:

| Worker Change | Fan Reaction | Fanbase Variation |
|---------------|--------------|-------------------|
| Stays humble despite success | Increased loyalty, "deserves it" | Universal positive |
| Becomes arrogant/political | Backlash, "sold out" accusations | Smarks turn fastest |
| Declines gracefully | Respect, appreciation, farewell tours | Universal positive |
| Refuses to step aside | "Hogging spotlight" resentment | Young fan resentment highest |
| Genuine reinvention | Renewed interest, "evolution" praise | Smarks appreciate most |
| Desperate clinging to past | Pity or mockery | Attitude Era fans mock hardest |

## Stage Transition Triggers

What causes workers to move between stages:

| Transition | Common Triggers |
|------------|-----------------|
| Rookie ‚Üí Breakout | Hot angle, viral moment, miracle match, political opening |
| Breakout ‚Üí Established | Sustained push, title reign, consistent quality |
| Established ‚Üí Peak | Dominant run, industry-defining moment, crossover success |
| Peak ‚Üí Decline | Age, injuries, staleness, new stars rising |
| Any ‚Üí Crisis | Major scandal, career-threatening injury, public meltdown |
| Crisis ‚Üí Reinvention | Successful return, persona change, redemption booking |
| Decline ‚Üí Legacy | Semi-retirement, special attraction booking, industry respect |
