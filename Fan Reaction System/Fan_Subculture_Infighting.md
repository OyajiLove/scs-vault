## 1. WHEN SUBCULTURE INFIGHTING BEGINS

| Trigger | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Golden Era Maturity** | As trust and emotional investment peak, subgroups start prioritizing different aspects of what made the boom great |
| **Booking Tone Drift** | If booking slowly shifts style (more humor, more shock, less prestige), loyalists fracture into camps |
| **Generational Drift** | New younger fans drawn during the boom may not value the same emotional structures as early adopters |
| **External Cultural Waves** | Shifts in broader film, music, political tone push newer emotional needs into fan consciousness |
| **Worker/Division Wars** | Different divisions (Heavyweights vs Juniors, Hardcore vs Technical) become proxy wars for fan identity |

### Trigger Thresholds

| Trigger | Threshold |
|---------|-----------|
| **Golden Era Duration** | 3+ years increases infighting probability |
| **Booking Consistency Drop** | Below 70% consistency triggers fractures |
| **Generational Gap** | 5+ years of new fan influx without cultural alignment |
| **Division Favoritism** | Significant resource imbalance between divisions |

## 2. SUBCULTURE TYPES THAT EMERGE

| Archetype | Traits | Loves | Hates |
|-----------|--------|-------|-------|
| **Purists** | Love prestige arcs, protected titles, serious emotional feuds | Long matches, clean finishes, storytelling | Crash TV, cheap swerves, overbooked finishes |
| **Crash TV Fans** | Chase chaos, blood, shock moments. Pop for unpredictability. | Weapons, surprises, car crashes | Slow builds, "boring" psychology |
| **Cynics/Ironists** | Cheer swerves, mock sincerity. Love when booking undercuts emotional investment. | Meta-commentary, "gotcha" moments | Sincere emotional moments, traditional heroes |
| **Workrate Fetishists** | Worship match quality over emotional storytelling. Title prestige secondary. | Technical skill, five-star classics | Character work, "sports entertainment" |
| **Cult Loyalists** | Idolize promotion itself over booking content. Will defend no matter what. | Anything their promotion does | Criticism, outsider perspectives |
| **Heat Chasers** | Worship brutal heel runs, betrayal arcs, dominant villains | Cruelty, "realism," heel dominance | Feel-good arcs, face-heavy booking |
| **Spectacle Addicts** | Want big entrances, wild gimmicks, larger-than-life aesthetics | Grand entrances, visual spectacle | Story depth, workrate focus |

## 3. SIMULATION MECHANICS

| Behavior | Effect |
|----------|--------|
| **Subculture Memory Formation** | Fans remember which eras, workers, divisions represented "their" vision of wrestling |
| **Subculture Size Growth/Shrinkage** | Booking decisions (title arcs, match types, division focus) empower or erode subgroups |
| **Booking Satisfaction Modifiers** | Booking that pleases one subgroup may alienate others. No longer possible to satisfy everyone easily. |
| **Internal Fan Wars** | Fanbase becomes divided: booing golden boys, hijacking shows, online discourse poisoning locker room morale, shifting merch sales, crowd reactions, ratings patterns |
| **Promotion Cultural Drift Risk** | If leadership panders too much to one subgroup, fanbase homogenizes and overall reach shrinks |

| Technical clinic, no drama | +10 | -15 | -10 | +25 | +5 | -15 | -20 |

## 4. INTERNAL FAN WAR MANIFESTATIONS

| Manifestation | Effect |
|---------------|--------|
| **Show Hijacking** | Fans chant against pushed workers, derail segments |
| **Golden Boy Backlash** | Workers pushed by one subculture get actively booed by others |
| **Online Discourse Poisoning** | Social media wars affect worker morale, booking confidence |
| **Merch Sales Fragmentation** | Different workers sell to different subcultures |
| **Ratings Pattern Shifts** | Segments targeting one group cause tune-out from others |
| **Live Crowd Division** | Dueling chants, split reactions, confused atmosphere |

### Hijacking Risk Factors

| Factor | Risk Increase |
|--------|---------------|
| **Large Cynical/Irony Population** | +30% hijack risk |
| **Recent Booking Disappointment** | +20% per major disappointment |
| **Smark-Heavy Venue** | +25% in certain markets |
| **Long Segment with Wrong Tone** | +15% per mismatch |

## 5. PROMOTION CULTURAL DRIFT RISK

When leadership panders too heavily to one subculture:

| Scenario | Outcome |
|----------|---------|
| **Workrate Echo Chamber (ROH 2000s)** | Critical darling status but shrinking audience, unable to break mainstream |
| **Crash TV Overload (Late WCW)** | Casual spike then mass exodus when novelty wears off |
| **Purist Fortress (Old NWA)** | Loyal but aging audience, unable to attract new generations |
| **Spectacle Addiction (Late WCW 2)** | Style over substance, fans stop caring about outcomes |

### Echo Chamber Warning Signs

| Sign | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| **Declining attendance despite critical praise** | Workrate echo chamber forming |
| **Spike then crash ratings pattern** | Crash TV burnout |
| **Aging demographic, no youth influx** | Purist fortress |
| **High production values, low emotional investment** | Spectacle addiction |

## 6. HISTORICAL FAN INFIGHTING EXAMPLES

| Promotion | Fracture |
|-----------|----------|
| **WWF Late Attitude Era** | Purists wanted Bret-style technical arcs; cynics embraced crash TV |
| **AEW 2022-2024** | Split between Elite loyalists, Hangman emotional story fans, Punk working-class realism fans, and spectacle/spotfest lovers |
| **NJPW Early 2000s** | Strong style loyalists vs MMA "Inokiism" fans. Bitter civil war killed trust for years. |
| **ECW Late Era** | Hardcore deathmatch fans vs technical wrestling fans (Lance Storm, Jerry Lynn audience) arguing over true ECW "spirit" |

### AEW Post-Brawl Out Model

| Fan Archetype | Loyalty |
|---------------|---------|
| **Hangman fans** | Emotionally driven cowboy storytelling lovers |
| **Elite fans** | Style-driven athletic art fans |
| **Punk fans** | Working-class realism fans |
| **Spotfest fans** | Crash TV spectacle lovers |
| **MJF fans** | Heat chasers and antihero lovers |

All emotionally diverging even inside the same shows. Booking that pleases one group causes backlash from another.

## 7. WORLD MEMORY EFFECTS

| Effect | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Fracture Memory** | Fanbase remembered historically as fractured or unified |
| **Blame Attribution** | Golden Era endings often blamed on "bad booking" but really caused by emotional splintering |
| **Worker Career Mapping** | Worker careers affected by which fan subgroup they emotionally captured |
| **Reunion Difficulty** | Once fractured, subcultures rarely fully reunite |
