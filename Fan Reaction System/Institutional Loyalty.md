## Core Concept

Fans develop loyalty to promotions as institutions, not just individual workers. This creates:

1. **Protective buffers** for scandals involving the promotion or its stars
2. **Tribal behavior** defending against outside criticism
3. **Blind spots** that allow misconduct to continue unchallenged
4. **Institutional rot** when repeated scandals accumulate

---

## Loyalty Levels

Fans exist on a spectrum of institutional loyalty:

| Level | Name | Description | % of Typical Fanbase |
|-------|------|-------------|---------------------|
| 1 | **Casual** | Watches when convenient, no strong attachment | 25-35% |
| 2 | **Regular** | Consistent viewer, mild defense of promotion | 30-40% |
| 3 | **Devoted** | Deeply invested, buys merch, attends shows, active online | 15-25% |
| 4 | **Tribal** | Promotion is part of identity; attacks critics; overlooks scandals | 8-15% |
| 5 | **Cult** | Promotion can do no wrong; conspiracy thinking about critics | 2-5% |

## Loyalty Level Behaviors

### Level 1: Casual

| Behavior | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| Scandal Response | React to scandal itself, no institutional protection |
| Criticism Response | Don't engage in defense |
| Consumption Pattern | Watch occasionally, skip often |
| Spending | Minimal (maybe one event/year) |
| Online Presence | Lurker or absent |

**Scandal Impact:** Full damage, no buffer

---

### Level 2: Regular

| Behavior | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| Scandal Response | Wait for promotion's official response before judging |
| Criticism Response | Mild pushback in conversations |
| Consumption Pattern | Weekly viewer, rarely miss big shows |
| Spending | Moderate (merch, some events) |
| Online Presence | Occasional engagement |

**Scandal Impact:** Slight delay in judgment, small buffer

---

### Level 3: Devoted

| Behavior | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| Scandal Response | Give benefit of doubt, seek exculpatory information |
| Criticism Response | Active defense online and in person |
| Consumption Pattern | Never miss shows, consume all content |
| Spending | High (merch, events, subscriptions, travel) |
| Online Presence | Daily engagement, community member |

**Scandal Impact:** Moderate protection, will eventually turn if evidence overwhelming

---

### Level 4: Tribal

| Behavior | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| Scandal Response | Defend worker/promotion regardless of evidence |
| Criticism Response | Attack critics personally, dismiss sources |
| Consumption Pattern | Promotion is lifestyle, not just entertainment |
| Spending | Very high (multiple events, collector merch) |
| Online Presence | Warrior for the brand, argues constantly |

**Scandal Impact:** Strong protection, only "murder-level" scandals break through

---

### Level 5: Cult

| Behavior | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| Scandal Response | Deny evidence, claim conspiracy against promotion |
| Criticism Response | Harassment campaigns, doxxing attempts, organized attacks |
| Consumption Pattern | Entire identity wrapped in promotion |
| Spending | Extreme (will go into debt for promotion) |
| Online Presence | Obsessive, parasocial, potentially dangerous |

**Scandal Impact:** Near-total immunity in their eyes; will never turn

## Institutional Protection Effect

Higher institutional loyalty creates scandal shielding:

### Protection Formula

```
Institutional Protection % = Œ£ (Loyalty Level Weight √ó % of Fanbase at Level)
```

Where Loyalty Level Weights are:
- Level 1 (Casual): 0%
- Level 2 (Regular): 10%

## Why This Matters (Real-World Truth)

This system models uncomfortable realities:

| Reality | System Implementation |
|---------|----------------------|
| **Big promotions survive repeated scandals** | High institutional loyalty buffers damage |
| **Small promotions die from single scandals** | Low institutional loyalty means full impact |
| **Fans defend the indefensible** | Tribal/Cult levels explain this behavior |
| **Cover-ups work (temporarily)** | Institutional loyalty delays backlash |
| **Some fans never turn** | Cult level represents the "ride or die" segment |

### Historical Examples

| Promotion | Scandal | Survival Factor |
|-----------|---------|-----------------|
| **WWE** | Multiple sexual misconduct scandals over decades | High institutional loyalty (25%+), global reach, no viable alternative for casuals |
| **WCW** | Backstage chaos, talent exodus | Institutional loyalty eroded as product declined; casuals left first |
| **ROH (2000s)** | Minor scandals | Strong indie hardcore loyalty protected them |
| **Small Indie X** | Single predator scandal | Zero institutional protection; immediate collapse |

## Institutional Rot

Repeated scandals accumulate even with high institutional loyalty:

### Rot Accumulation

| Event | Rot Added |
|-------|-----------|
| Minor scandal (covered up) | +5% |
| Minor scandal (exposed) | +8% |
| Moderate scandal (covered up) | +10% |
| Moderate scandal (exposed) | +15% |
| Major scandal (covered up, later exposed) | +25% |
| Major scandal (exposed immediately) | +20% |
| Cover-up exposed | Additional +10% |

### Rot Effects

| Rot Level | Effects |
|-----------|---------|
| 0-20% | Business as usual |
| 21-40% | Some sponsors nervous, media asks questions, devoted fans start questioning |
| 41-60% | Sponsor pulls begin, recruitment harder, morale drops, regular fans drift |
| 61-80% | Major sponsors flee, talent exodus begins, tribal fans crack |
| 81-100% | Business collapse or forced ownership change; only cult remains |

### Rot Decay

Rot can decay over time if no new scandals occur:

| Time Without Scandal | Rot Decay |
|---------------------|-----------|
| 1 year | -5% |
| 2 years | -8% |
| 5 years | -15% |
| 10 years | -25% |

Active reputation management (PR campaigns, charity work, reforms) can accelerate decay by 50%.

## Fanbase Type and Institutional Loyalty

Different fanbase types are more or less prone to institutional loyalty:

| Fanbase Type | Institutional Loyalty Tendency | Notes |
|--------------|-------------------------------|-------|
| Hardcore Company Loyalists | Very High | By definition, loyal to institution |
| Indie Hardcore Fans | Low | Loyal to workers/workrate, not brands |
| Family-Friendly Fans | Medium | Loyal to "safe" brands |
| Deathmatch Outlaw Fans | Very Low | Follow chaos, not institutions |
| Lucha Traditionalists | High | Loyal to traditional institutions (CMLL, etc.) |
| Joshi Superfans | Medium-High | Loyal to specific promotions with "their" workers |
| Edgy Attitude Era Types | Medium | Nostalgic loyalty to "their" era's promotion |
| Casual Mainstream Fans | Low | No strong attachment |
| Smark/Internet Fans | Low-Medium | Critical but can develop ironic loyalty |
