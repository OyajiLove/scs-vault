## Institutional Protection Reality

**Core Truth:** Big promotions and big stars survive scandals that would destroy smaller entities.

| Reality | System Implementation |
|---------|----------------------|
| Big Stars Survive Longer | Top 1-5% names shielded by management, fans, and media bias |
| Big Promotions Survive | WWE/NJPW-level companies take PR hits but barely budge revenue |
| Fans Are Willing to Ignore | Loyalty blinds large chunks of fanbases; only sustained heat cracks it |
| Smaller Promotions Die Quicker | Indies can't survive medium scandals involving ownership or champions |
| Power Brokers Know and Hide | Executives often protect or cover up scandals internally (until leak) |

### Scandal Shielding Mechanic

Major stars and promotions have internal "cover up" chances that temporarily delay backlash:

| Entity | Cover-Up Chance | Duration |
|--------|-----------------|----------|
| Enhancement talent | 0% | N/A |
| Midcarder | 10% | 1-2 weeks |
| Main eventer | 30% | 1-3 months |
| Top star | 50% | 3-12 months |
| Company owner | 70% | Years (until leak) |

If cover-up fails or scandal eventually surfaces, the backlash is often WORSE due to the cover-up itself.

---

## Institutional Rot

Promotions can survive individual scandals, but repeated scandals accumulate:

| Rot Level | Effects |
|-----------|---------|
| 0-20% | Business as usual |
| 21-40% | Some sponsors nervous, media asks questions |
| 41-60% | Sponsor pulls, recruitment harder, morale drops |
| 61-80% | Major sponsors flee, talent exodus begins |
| 81-100% | Business collapse or forced ownership change |

Each covered-up scandal that eventually surfaces adds +10-20% to institutional rot.
