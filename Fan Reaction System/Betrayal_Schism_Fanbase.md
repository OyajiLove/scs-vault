## 1. SCHISM CAUSES (4 TRIGGERS)

| Cause | Description | Severity |
|-------|-------------|----------|
| **Golden Era Collapse** | Emotional anchors (promotions, workers, booking philosophies) destroyed or betrayed | SEVERE |
| **Worker Exodus** | Beloved stars defect to rivals or launch splinter promotions, forcing fans to emotionally choose sides | HIGH |
| **Corporate Takeover or Collapse** | Takeovers (e.g., Vince buying Georgia Championship Wrestling's TBS slot: Black Saturday) alienate loyalists | SEVERE |
| **Philosophical Betrayals** | Promotions abandoning their founding emotional principles for corporate expansion, crash TV, or spectacle | HIGH |

### Black Saturday Case Study

**Event:** Vince McMahon purchases GCW's TBS timeslot (1984)

**What Happened:** TBS fans expecting serious Georgia Championship Wrestling instead get Vince's WWF cartoon product.

**Fan Response:**
- Massive emotional betrayal and trauma
- Trust in TBS wrestling programming destroyed
- Regional loyalty shattered
- Many fans never returned to wrestling

**Legacy:** Canonical example of corporate takeover schism. "Black Saturday" remains infamous decades later.

## 2. FAN REACTION TYPES (5 RESPONSES)

| Reaction | Behavior | Percentage (Typical) |
|----------|----------|---------------------|
| **Loyalist Stagnation** | Hardcore fans emotionally refuse to move on: clinging to old logos, old belts, even after home fed is gutted | 10-20% |
| **Emotional Exodus** | Fans emotionally migrate to new promotions preserving the spirit they loved (if any exist) | 25-40% |
| **Cynical Withdrawal** | Fans disengage completely from wrestling emotionally: bitterness, anger, loss of trust | 15-30% |
| **Anarchist Redirection** | Some fans embrace chaos: flipping allegiance to anything subversive, punk, hardcore, even in rejection of old emotional anchors | 10-20% |
| **Betrayal Martyrdom** | Workers who resist betrayal (Kawada staying, Arn Anderson staying WCW) gain emotional myth status for loyalists | N/A (worker reaction, not fan) |

### Reaction Distribution by Schism Type

| Schism Type | Stagnation | Exodus | Withdrawal | Anarchism |
|-------------|------------|--------|------------|-----------|
| **Golden Era Collapse** | 25% | 30% | 30% | 15% |
| **Worker Exodus** | 15% | 45% | 20% | 20% |
| **Corporate Takeover** | 20% | 25% | 40% | 15% |
| **Philosophical Betrayal** | 20% | 35% | 25% | 20% |

## 3. SIMULATION MECHANICS

| Mechanic | Behavior |
|----------|----------|
| **Emotional Trust Collapse** | Betrayed fans no longer trust brand narratives easily. Rebuilding loyalty much harder even after years. |
| **Subculture Splintering** | Fan cultures fracture: Traditionalists vs Modernists, Purists vs Anarchists, Nostalgists vs Pragmatists |
| **Mythological Memory Drift** | Over time, splintered fanbases remember betrayals differently: some mythologize defiance, others resent rebellion |

### Trust Rebuild Difficulty

| Initial Trust | Post-Schism Trust | Rebuild Rate |
|---------------|-------------------|--------------|
| 80+ | 20-40 | Very Slow (5+ years) |
| 60-79 | 15-35 | Slow (3-5 years) |
| 40-59 | 10-30 | Medium (2-3 years) |
| <40 | 5-25 | Fast (1-2 years) |

### Subculture Splinter Patterns

| Schism Type | Primary Split |
|-------------|---------------|
| **Golden Era Collapse** | Nostalgists vs Pragmatists |
| **Worker Exodus** | Loyalty factions (follow worker vs stay home) |
| **Corporate Takeover** | Purists vs Corporate Accepters |
| **Philosophical Betrayal** | Traditionalists vs Modernists |

## 4. HISTORICAL MODELS

| Event | Fanbase Schism |
|-------|----------------|
| **Black Saturday (1984)** | TBS fans expecting serious Georgia Championship Wrestling see Vince's WWF cartoon product. Massive emotional betrayal and trauma. Many fans never returned. |
| **NOAH Split (2000)** | AJPW loyalists emotionally fractured: some mourned lost AJPW soul, others migrated to NOAH for King's Road survival. Schism never fully healed. |
| **AEW Brawl Out Fallout (2022)** | Early AEW fanbase fragmented: Punk loyalists vs Elite loyalists vs Emotional Realists. Online discourse became factional warfare. |
| **WCW Death (2001)** | Fans scattered: some accepted WWE absorption, some withdrew bitterly, some became permanent WWE critics, some followed workers to TNA/indies |

### AEW Brawl Out Case Study

**Event:** Backstage altercation at All Out 2022

**Schism Cause:** Worker exodus (Punk departure) + Philosophical betrayal (what is AEW's identity?)

**Fan Factions Formed:**
- **Punk Loyalists:** "Punk was right, Elite politics ruined AEW"
- **Elite Loyalists:** "Punk was the problem, Elite built this"
- **Emotional Realists:** "Hangman/Swerve storytelling is the real AEW"
- **Cynical Withdrawers:** "AEW is just as bad as WWE now"

**Ongoing Effects:**
- Online discourse remains factional
- Crowd reactions split on key figures
- Booking decisions scrutinized through factional lenses
- Trust in "backstage harmony" narrative damaged

## 5. BETRAYAL MARTYRDOM

Workers who resist betrayal gain special status:

| Martyr Type | Description | Example |
|-------------|-------------|---------|
| **Last Samurai** | Stayed when everyone else fled, endured decay | Kawada post-NOAH split |
| **Loyal Soldier** | Remained with company through crisis despite better options | Arn Anderson WCW loyalty |
| **Principled Exile** | Left on principle, not for money, gained respect | Cody Rhodes AEW departure (initial perception) |
| **Tragic Holdout** | Stayed too long, career damaged, but respected for loyalty | Various territory era workers |

**Martyrdom Effects:**
- +20-40 respect from loyalist fans
- Historical narrative favor in long-term memory
- Homecoming potential if company recovers
- Cautionary tale status if company dies

## 7. PHILOSOPHY

> "When the thing you loved betrays what made you love it, you don't just lose a promotion. You lose a piece of yourself that believed. Some fans rebuild that belief somewhere else. Some never do. And some spend the rest of their lives fighting over whose fault it was."

Fanbase schisms create lasting emotional wounds. They're not just business disruptions: they're identity crises for communities built around shared love.
