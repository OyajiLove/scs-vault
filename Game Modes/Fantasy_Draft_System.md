## Core Concept

Fantasy drafts in SCS are player-initiated simulations. You start with a pool of workers (real, fictional, mixed, historical) and either solo or with other players, draft rosters to build new promotions.

It's creative, chaotic, and fun as hell, but still grounded in SCS emotional memory.

| Mode | Context |
|------|---------|
| **Creative Mode (Single Player)** | Build a new promotion from scratch using a mixed pool of legends, up-and-comers, and cult favourites |
| **Multiplayer Draft** | You and friends draft against each other in a live or asynchronous environment, each building a unique promotion |

## Fantasy Draft Mechanics ‚úÖ LOCKED

| Feature | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| **Draft Pools** | Any mix of real, fictional, or AI-generated workers. Full control of active roster pool. |
| **Peak Draft Option** | Draft workers in their all-time peak versions. Attributes boosted accordingly. |
| **Draft Styles** | Traditional (snake draft), randomized, auction, protect/unprotect systems |
| **Package Deals** | Road Warriors, Steiners, Hardy Boyz, Midnight Express with Cornette. Smart package logic. Player prompted to draft together or risk morale hits. |
| **Live Reactions** | AI workers react in real time to being picked, passed over, drafted solo, or drafted with stables. Commentators cover shocks (e.g., Hogan slipping deep into the draft). |
| **Custom Rules** | Max roster size, salary caps, mandatory archetype diversity (must draft a Technician, a Brawler, etc.). Only 1 mythic legend per promotion. Only 2 current champions. Must take 3 unpushed midcarders per 10 picks. |
| **Protect Lists** | Existing promotions can protect top stars during real-world era drafts (optional) |
| **Hidden Gems** | Some workers marked as underrated have breakout potential post-draft if booked properly. Scouting reports may mislead (Austin example). |
| **Draft Position Memory** | Draft position affects ego, loyalty, morale (picked 45th = chip on shoulder arc) |
| **Tag Team & Stable Integrity** | Breaking historic bonds (Animal without Hawk) causes backstage morale crashes. Optional toggle: draft together (preserves loyalty web) or draft separately (fractures locker room). |
| **Worker Resistance** | Rare cases where workers refuse to sign after being drafted depending on loyalty, ego, and fit (Brody doesn't want to work for New York) |
| **Post-Draft Genesis** | Game generates initial feuds, belts, alliances, backstage dynamics immediately after draft based on choices |

### Trade Evaluation System (AI)

| Factor | AI Behavior |
|--------|-------------|
| **Perceived Value** | Skill ratings, draw power, emotional memory (big matches, betrayals), current push status |
| **Needs-Based Logic** | Roster gaps: style, star power, locker room stability |
| **Promotion Personality** | Conservative feds avoid complex trades. Aggressive/chaotic feds throw dice. |
| **Ego & Pride** | High Ego owners may reject deals to avoid "looking weak." Low Loyalty = cutthroat. |
| **Desperation Modifier** | Poor ratings/morale = suddenly open to losing key stars for picks/cash |
| **Rivalry Tension** | Promotions with heat refuse trades unless offer is too good. Personal history overrides logic. |

**AI is emotional and biased, not perfectly optimized, but believable.**

### Draft Busts and Late Bloomers (Clarified)

‚ùå **What We're NOT Doing:**
- No fixed ceilings or "hard caps"
- No "D+ Potential" destiny rolls like TEW
- No predetermined career limits

‚úÖ **What We ARE Doing:**
Busts and late bloomers emerge from:
1. Hidden ratings (Drive, Adaptability, Composure, Ego, Loyalty)
2. Booking context (soul fit, match success, crowd reaction, locker room)
3. Fan/media narrative (hype vs delivery)
4. Who believed in them and what stories were told

| Trait | Effect |
|-------|--------|
| **Drive** | High = more likely to evolve, improve, push for greatness |
| **Adaptability** | High = adjusts to fed's style, locker room, match flow |
| **Composure** | High = handles setbacks, late draft, slow pushes |
| **Booking Fit** | Contextual flag, bad fit = early awkwardness unless booking adjusts |

**Narratives are reversible. Workers who start cold can heat up years later. Workers with high traits can still flop if booked badly.**

### Draft Memory Fallout

| Effect | Impact |
|--------|--------|
| **Passed Over** | Worker remembers who ignored them. Promos reference it. May refuse future contracts. |
| **Drafted by Enemy** | May cause drama, morale collapse, or politic for trade |
| **Undrafted** | Morale hits. May go indie/international. Some retire bitter. Some reinvent as monsters. |
| **Late Round Gems** | Promotions that hit on "steals" get Fan Trust Boost, reputation as smart booking team |
| **Trade Regret Arcs** | "You gave up him for this?!" = long-term emotional baggage fans remember |

**Draft is not a phase, it's an origin story.**

## Trades Outside Draft (Vol 5 line 1065) ‚úÖ LOCKED

Trades exist at all times, not just on Draft Day.

| Trade Type | Notes |
|------------|-------|
| **Worker-for-Worker Swaps** | Can be public (kayfabe) or behind-the-scenes |
| **Worker + Cash for Pick** | Feds sell contracts, often when financially desperate |
| **Contract Buyouts** | Cash-only or talent swap deals for locked contracts |
| **Trade Refusals** | Worker may refuse to report if they hate the destination |
| **Rival Trade Friction** | Harder to negotiate with bitter rivals unless lopsided deal |
| **Trade Motivation Tags** | Offload high-ego workers, cash in on hot talent, fill morale void post-scandal |
| **Scouting Tag Influence** | High-scouting staff better evaluate under-the-radar targets or avoid bad fits |

**Fully dynamic system tied to loyalty, ego, negotiation skill, and world emotional history.**
