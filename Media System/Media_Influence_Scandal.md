## 1. CORE MEDIA POWERS (4 FORCES)

| Media Force | Power | Historical Peak |
|-------------|-------|-----------------|
| **Magazines/Newspapers** | Could elevate a promotion without TV access (e.g., FMW early 90s). Created heroes, myths, betrayals based on who they covered and how. | Japan: 1970s-2000s; US: 1970s-1980s |
| **Photographer Cartels** | Exclusive photo access controlled public visual memory of workers, angles, betrayals, and feuds | Japan: 1960s-1990s |
| **Writers/Editors as Kingmakers** | Writers like Tarzan Yamamoto could make or break new promotions or cement workers' early myths | Throughout wrestling history |
| **Newspaper Legitimacy** | Wrestlers getting real sports page coverage (especially in Japan) created massive mainstream emotional legitimacy | Japan: 1950s-present; US: Limited |

### Regional Media Importance

| Region | Primary Media | Secondary Media | Media Power Level |
|--------|---------------|-----------------|-------------------|
| **Japan** | Weekly Pro Wrestling, Weekly Gong, Tokyo Sports | Newspapers (sports pages) | CRITICAL (treated as real sport) |
| **US (Pre-1990)** | Apter Mags (PWI, etc.) | Regional newspapers | HIGH (kayfabe protection) |
| **US (Post-1990)** | Dirt sheets, Internet | TV dominates | MEDIUM (TV supersedes print) |
| **Mexico** | Box y Lucha, regional papers | Radio | HIGH (lucha as cultural institution) |

## 2. MEDIA BEHAVIOR TYPES (4 ARCHETYPES)

| Type | Description | Growth Effect | Risk Level |
|------|-------------|---------------|------------|
| **Neutral Chroniclers** | Attempt honest coverage (rare). Build emotional authenticity slowly but less explosively. | Slow, steady | LOW |
| **Loyalist Boosters** | Favor certain promotions/workers by ideology or bribes. Faster growth, but massive scandal risk. | Fast, volatile | HIGH |
| **Outlaw Advocates** | Small niche media backing outsider movements (deathmatch feds, indie coalitions, rebellions) | Niche but loyal | MEDIUM |
| **Corporate Sellouts** | Magazines/media drift into safe PR tools for dominant promotions. Loyalty fracture triggers. | Stable but hollow | MEDIUM |

## 3. SIMULATION MECHANICS

| Mechanic | Behavior |
|----------|----------|
| **Media Affiliation Rolls** | Promotions/workers can gain media loyalty, boosting reputation, match exposure, and golden myth creation |
| **Media Scandal Risk** | Bribery, corruption, blacklisting scandals can erupt, damaging promotion trust and accelerating fan disillusionment |
| **Rediscovery Risk** | If a promotion historically boosted by media collapses or betrays ideals, hardcore fan historians may expose early media manipulation later |
| **Fanbase Drift Response** | Fans who discover media manipulation may emotionally defect or become cynical about prior myths |

### Media Affiliation Benefits

| Affiliation Level | Benefits |
|-------------------|----------|
| **Hostile** | Negative coverage, reputation damage, blackout risk |
| **Neutral** | Fair coverage, no bonus or penalty |
| **Friendly** | Positive coverage, +10% reputation growth |
| **Loyalist** | Puff pieces, myth creation, +25% reputation growth, scandal risk |
| **Owned/Bribed** | Full narrative control, +40% reputation growth, MAJOR scandal risk |

## 4. MEDIA SCANDAL TYPES

| Scandal | Effect | Severity |
|---------|--------|----------|
| **Bribery Exposed** | Fanbase loyalty erosion for the promoted company. Media outlet credibility damaged. | HIGH |
| **Blacklist Exposed** | Sympathy boost for exiled workers/factions. Rebel movements born. | MEDIUM-HIGH |
| **Fabricated Stories Exposed** | Promotion's emotional aura ("legitimacy," "purity," "rebellion") collapses partially or completely | HIGH |
| **Photo Manipulation Exposed** | Myths about feuds or betrayals crack. Historical memory re-evaluated. | MEDIUM |

### Scandal Fallout Timeline

| Phase | Duration | Effects |
|-------|----------|---------|
| **Immediate** | 0-3 months | Trust collapse, media coverage chaos, fan outrage |
| **Short-term** | 3-12 months | Reputation damage crystallizes, some fans leave |
| **Long-term** | 1-5 years | Historical memory shifts, new narratives form |
| **Permanent** | Forever | Scandal becomes part of promotion/worker's legacy |

## 5. HISTORICAL MODELS

| Event | Media Impact |
|-------|--------------|
| **Tarzan Yamamoto Scandal (FMW)** | FMW's early legitimacy shaken when preferential coverage exposed. Hurt brand emotional credibility badly. Showed media manipulation could backfire catastrophically. |
| **AJPW/NJPW Magazine Loyalty (80s-90s)** | Positive magazine bias helped preserve "sports legitimacy" myths even during backstage chaos eras. Created golden era narratives. |
| **Apter Mag Era (US, 70s-80s)** | Pro Wrestling Illustrated boosted kayfabe. Workers' emotional reputation enhanced or crippled based on coverage. Created parallel "legitimate" rankings. |
| **Post-AEW Brawl Out Media Battles** | Wrestling media ecosystem splits: outlets tied to Elite vs Punk narratives, shaping future fan loyalty fractures. Modern example of media-driven narrative war. |

### FMW/Tarzan Yamamoto Case Study

**Setup:** FMW paid Tarzan Yamamoto (famous Weekly Pro Wrestling editor) for preferential coverage to grow without TV.

**Initial Effect:** FMW gained legitimacy and exposure far beyond their actual resources. Magazine coverage treated them seriously.

**Scandal Trigger:** Payment arrangement exposed.

**Fallout:**
- FMW's emotional credibility damaged
- Fans questioned whether early greatness was real or manufactured
- Historical memory of early FMW permanently tainted
- Other promotions' media relationships scrutinized

**Lesson:** Media manipulation can accelerate growth but creates existential scandal risk.
