## Overview

The media landscape changes dramatically across eras. What's possible in 2020 (instant viral exposure) was unthinkable in 1985 (local newspaper coverage). Era Media Landscape defines the rules of media interaction for each time period.

---

## Era Definitions

### Territory Era (1950-1984)

**Media Landscape:**
- Local newspapers primary coverage
- Regional TV (no national cable)
- Dirtsheets exist but tiny circulation
- Kayfabe strongly protected by mainstream

| Element | Status |
|---------|--------|
| **Kayfabe Protection** | Very High (95%) |
| **Scandal Spread Speed** | Very Slow (weeks/months) |
| **Dirtsheet Reach** | Minimal (<5% of fans) |
| **Worker Privacy** | High |
| **Fan Communication** | Mail, local gatherings |
| **Story Control** | High (fed controls narrative) |

**Media Interactions:**
- Newspaper interviews (kayfabe)
- Local TV appearances (in character)
- Magazine profiles (protected)
- Rare mainstream crossover

### National Expansion (1985-1994)

**Media Landscape:**
- National TV emerges (cable)
- Dirtsheets grow (Wrestling Observer)
- Mainstream interest increases
- Kayfabe cracks begin

| Element | Status |
|---------|--------|
| **Kayfabe Protection** | High but eroding (80%) |
| **Scandal Spread Speed** | Slow-Medium (days/weeks) |
| **Dirtsheet Reach** | Growing (10-15% of hardcore fans) |
| **Worker Privacy** | Medium |
| **Fan Communication** | Mail, hotlines, conventions |
| **Story Control** | Medium-High |

**Media Interactions:**
- National TV talk shows
- Mainstream magazine features
- Dirtsheet subscriptions
- Early "worked shoot" exposure

### Monday Night Wars (1995-2001)

**Media Landscape:**
- Peak mainstream attention
- Internet emerges (websites, forums)
- Dirtsheets go online
- Kayfabe openly questioned

| Element | Status |
|---------|--------|
| **Kayfabe Protection** | Medium (50%) |
| **Scandal Spread Speed** | Medium (hours/days) |
| **Dirtsheet Reach** | Significant (20-30% of fans) |
| **Worker Privacy** | Low-Medium |
| **Fan Communication** | Internet forums, chat rooms |
| **Story Control** | Medium (spoilers leak) |

**Media Interactions:**
- Major talk show appearances
- Entertainment media coverage
- Website exclusives
- Newsletter scoops
- Early viral moments (video clips)

### Reality Era (2002-2010)

**Media Landscape:**
- Post-Attitude decline
- Internet dominates fan discourse
- YouTube changes distribution
- Kayfabe acknowledged as fiction

| Element | Status |
|---------|--------|
| **Kayfabe Protection** | Low (30%) |
| **Scandal Spread Speed** | Fast (hours) |
| **Dirtsheet Reach** | High (50%+ of engaged fans) |
| **Worker Privacy** | Low |
| **Fan Communication** | Forums, early social media |
| **Story Control** | Low (everything leaks) |

**Media Interactions:**
- Mainstream rarely interested
- Internet site exclusives
- YouTube shoot interviews
- Wikipedia/fan wikis as record
- Early podcasts

### Social Media Era (2011-present)

**Media Landscape:**
- Workers control own platforms
- Instant global reach
- Virality determines visibility
- Kayfabe impossible to maintain

| Element | Status |
|---------|--------|
| **Kayfabe Protection** | Minimal (10%) |
| **Scandal Spread Speed** | Instant (minutes) |
| **Dirtsheet Reach** | Universal access |
| **Worker Privacy** | Very Low |
| **Fan Communication** | Direct with workers |
| **Story Control** | Minimal (fans shape narrative) |

**Media Interactions:**
- Twitter/Instagram as primary platform
- Podcast circuit
- Streaming interviews
- Viral clips on TikTok/YouTube
- Fan harassment potential
- Worker shoots go immediate

## Era-Specific Mechanics

### Territory Era (1985 Default)

**Scandal Containment:** Very possible
- Local scandals stay local
- Mainstream won't report kayfabe-breaking
- Fed can bury stories

**Pop Growth:** Slow and steady
- No viral moments
- Regional building required
- National exposure rare

**Media Management:** Simple
- Control interviews
- Work with friendly journalists
- Dirtsheets largely ignorable

### Social Media Era

**Scandal Containment:** Nearly impossible
- Everything is instant
- Workers can't be silenced
- Fan archaeology uncovers history

**Pop Growth:** Volatile
- Viral moments create instant stars
- Also instant cancellation
- Constant content required

**Media Management:** Complex
- Every worker is a media outlet
- Fan discourse shapes perception
- Damage control extremely difficult

## Social Media Age Mechanics (#124)

**Core Concept:** "Fans in 2025 care about real-life character, authenticity, and social media presence."

### Era-Sensitive Information Spread

| Era | Information Speed | Containment Possibility |
|-----|-------------------|------------------------|
| **1970s** | Local papers only | Easy to squash |
| **1990s** | Days to weeks | Hard to contain |
| **2020s** | Minutes | Instant viral bomb, impossible to contain |

### Scandal Virality

Same scandal has different impacts based on era:
- **1985:** Local coverage, easily managed
- **2000:** Dirtsheet story, moderate spread
- **2020s:** Viral Twitter outrage, calls for firing, merch sales plummet instantly

### Push Backlash Mechanics

Fast-push with low quality in social media era:
- Fans revolt hard
- Hashtags start (#FireBooker, #WeDontWantX)
- Company forced to respond or double down
- Doubling down risks deeper backlash

### Worker Social Media Tags

| Tag | Effect |
|-----|--------|
| **Heavy Social Media User** | Increased chance of social media-based stories, higher scandal exposure |
| **Shuns Social Media** | Won't be involved in social media events, lower scandal exposure |
| **Social Media Savvy** | Can spin narratives, damage control bonus |
| **Chronically Online** | Creates drama, generates heat but risks scandals |

### Sponsor Reactions

| Era | Sponsor Response to Scandal |
|-----|-----------------------------|
| **Pre-1990s** | Mostly unaffected, wrestling had few sponsors |
| **1990s** | Some pullback on major scandals |
| **Post-2000s** | Pull money immediately if scandals taint brand |
