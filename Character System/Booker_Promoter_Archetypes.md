## Core Concept

Every booker and promoter has a **booking personality** that shapes how they:
- Structure feuds (slow burn vs hot shot)
- Handle title prestige (protect vs pop)
- Respond to crowd feedback (adapt vs stubborn)
- Take risks (conservative vs chaotic)
- Manage worker resistance (negotiate vs crush)

**Archetypes are not destiny.** They can drift over time based on pressure, success, failure, or scandal.

### The Architect
**Philosophy:** Slow burn. Long-term planning. Protect the story.

| Behavior | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| **Feud Pacing** | Builds feuds over months. Resists hotshot temptation. |
| **Title Booking** | Protects belt prestige. Long reigns. Meaningful challenges. |
| **Risk Tolerance** | Low. Prefers proven formulas. |
| **Self-Correction** | Slow to change. Trusts the plan even when crowd cools. |
| **Weakness** | Can bore crowds if pacing too slow. May miss hot moments. |

**Real Examples:** Gedo (peak NJPW), Dusty Rhodes (territory era), Triple H (NXT)

---

### The Heat Seeker
**Philosophy:** Strike while the iron is hot. Push what's working NOW.

| Behavior | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| **Feud Pacing** | Pushes hot feuds fast. Peaks early. |
| **Title Booking** | Quick switches to capitalize on heat. |
| **Risk Tolerance** | High. Chases crowd reactions aggressively. |
| **Self-Correction** | Overcorrects constantly based on recent pops. |
| **Weakness** | Burns out feuds. Creates boom/bust cycles. |

**Real Examples:** Vince Russo, peak Attitude Era WWF

---

### The Despot
**Philosophy:** My vision. My way. The crowd will learn to love it.

| Behavior | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| **Feud Pacing** | Books what they want regardless of crowd response. |
| **Title Booking** | Puts belt on favorites even if crowd rejects them. |
| **Risk Tolerance** | Medium. Takes risks on their vision, not crowd whims. |
| **Self-Correction** | Stubborn. Keeps pushing into the storm. |
| **Weakness** | Can kill feds if vision is wrong. Creates resentment. |

**Real Examples:** Vince McMahon (Roman push era), Kevin Sullivan (WCW)

---

### The Idealist
**Philosophy:** Wrestling should be THIS. Protect the art.

| Behavior | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| **Feud Pacing** | Books for "wrestling quality" over crowd heat. |
| **Title Booking** | Puts belt on "deserving" workers, sometimes ignoring draw. |
| **Risk Tolerance** | Low for mainstream appeal, high for artistic vision. |
| **Self-Correction** | Stubborn about principles. Won't compromise the art. |
| **Weakness** | May book critically acclaimed shows that don't draw. |

**Real Examples:** Bill Watts (purist era), some indie bookers

---

### The Fan Chaser
**Philosophy:** Give the people what they want. Always.

| Behavior | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| **Feud Pacing** | Reactive. Changes based on crowd response. |
| **Title Booking** | Switches based on who's over RIGHT NOW. |
| **Risk Tolerance** | High. Will pivot on a dime. |
| **Self-Correction** | Overcorrects aggressively. Whiplash booking. |
| **Weakness** | No long-term vision. Exhausts roster chasing trends. |

**Real Examples:** Late-era WCW, some modern booking

---

### The Politicker
**Philosophy:** Keep the locker room happy. Avoid conflict.

| Behavior | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| **Feud Pacing** | Books to satisfy backstage relationships. |
| **Title Booking** | Belt goes to whoever has most political pull. |
| **Risk Tolerance** | Low. Avoids booking that creates backstage heat. |
| **Self-Correction** | Changes based on who complains loudest. |
| **Weakness** | Talent with pull gets pushed regardless of over-ness. Glass cannons get buried. |

**Real Examples:** Some territory bookers, politically captured regimes

---

### The Trend Rider
**Philosophy:** What's hot in the industry? Let's do that.

| Behavior | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| **Feud Pacing** | Copies what's working elsewhere. |
| **Title Booking** | Mimics successful title strategies from competitors. |
| **Risk Tolerance** | Medium. Will try trends but abandons fast if they fail. |
| **Self-Correction** | Pivots to next trend when current one cools. |
| **Weakness** | No original identity. Fed soul drifts constantly. |

**Real Examples:** TNA (various eras), copycat promotions

---

### The Survivor
**Philosophy:** Protect what we have. Don't risk the core.

| Behavior | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| **Feud Pacing** | Conservative. Relies on proven main eventers. |
| **Title Booking** | Keeps belt on safe hands. Avoids risky transitions. |
| **Risk Tolerance** | Very low. Fears change. |
| **Self-Correction** | Only changes when forced by crisis. |
| **Weakness** | Stagnation. New talent can't break through. |

**Real Examples:** Declining territories, risk-averse regimes

---

### The Reactionary
**Philosophy:** Something's not working? Change EVERYTHING.

| Behavior | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| **Feud Pacing** | Shuffles feuds constantly after any dip. |
| **Title Booking** | Panic switches. Hot-shots when nervous. |
| **Risk Tolerance** | Chaotic. Swings between conservative and reckless. |
| **Self-Correction** | Overreacts to every data point. |
| **Weakness** | No consistency. Workers and fans can't trust the direction. |

**Real Examples:** Panicking regimes, owners who interfere

## Promoter/Owner Archetypes

Owners have their own archetypes that interact with (or override) their booker's style:

| Archetype | Behavior |
|-----------|----------|
| **Hands-Off** | Trusts the booker. Rarely interferes. |
| **Micromanager** | Overrides booker constantly. Their vision dominates. |
| **Money-First** | Booking decisions filtered through profit lens. |
| **Legacy Builder** | Wants to create something that outlasts them. Long-term focus. |
| **Ego-Driven** | Wants to be seen as the genius. Takes credit, avoids blame. |
| **Crisis Manager** | Only engages when things are falling apart. |

## Archetype Drift âœ… LOCKED

**Enabled by default.** Archetypes can evolve over time based on:

| Trigger | Drift Direction |
|---------|-----------------|
| **Sustained Success** | May become more confident (Despot drift) or complacent (Survivor drift) |
| **Sustained Failure** | May become desperate (Reactionary drift) or stubborn (Despot drift) |
| **Scandal** | May become paranoid (Politicker drift) or defiant (Despot drift) |
| **New Influence** | Hiring a strong #2 can shift archetype toward their style |
| **Generational Shift** | Aging bookers may drift toward Survivor or Idealist |

**Drift is tracked in world memory. Commentary may note "philosophy changes."**

**Player can intervene (or embrace it) during major crises or soul shifts.**
