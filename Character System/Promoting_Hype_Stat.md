## Core Concept

Promoting/Hype measures how well an owner or promoter can:
- Generate buzz for shows and workers
- Sell tickets regardless of card quality
- Manage PR and media relationships
- Build and maintain brand identity
- Attract sponsors and media coverage

**Key distinction:** This is separate from Booking Instinct. You can be a genius booker who can't sell tickets, or a carnival barker who packs houses with garbage.

---

## Scale: 0-20 ‚úÖ LOCKED

| Range | Behavior |
|-------|----------|
| **1-5** | Can't draw flies. Empty arenas. Media ignores the fed. Workers embarrassed to be associated. |
| **6-10** | Functional. Can fill small venues. Local media coverage. Struggles against competition. |
| **11-15** | Strong. Consistent gates. Regional buzz. Media seeks them out for quotes. |
| **16-20** | Legendary promoter. Creates events that feel like history. Sells out on name alone. Media treats every show as must-see. |

---

## What It Affects ‚úÖ LOCKED

- **Ticket Sales:** Direct modifier on attendance
- **Brand Buzz:** How much the fed is talked about in media/fan circles
- **PR Success:** Ability to spin scandals, build narratives, control story
- **Media Coverage:** Likelihood of press, interviews, mainstream attention
- **Sponsor Interest:** Higher hype = more lucrative deals
- **Worker Recruitment:** Hot feds attract talent; cold feds struggle

---

## Hype vs Reality Tension

| Scenario | Result |
|----------|--------|
| **High Hype + Good Booking** | Golden era. Packed houses, satisfied fans, growing legend. |
| **High Hype + Bad Booking** | Short-term success, long-term trust erosion. Fans feel burned. |
| **Low Hype + Good Booking** | Hidden gem fed. Cult following but struggles financially. |
| **Low Hype + Bad Booking** | Death spiral. No one knows, no one cares. |
