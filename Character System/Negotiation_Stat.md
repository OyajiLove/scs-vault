## Core Concept

Negotiation measures how well someone can:
- Secure favorable contract terms
- Execute beneficial trades
- Poach talent from competitors
- Leverage relationships and information
- Close deals under pressure

**Who has it:**
- All Owners/Promoters
- All Bookers
- Some Workers (those with backstage political influence, veteran status, or agent roles)

---

## Scale: 0-20 ‚úÖ LOCKED

| Range | Behavior |
|-------|----------|
| **1-5** | Gets fleeced constantly. Signs bad contracts. Makes trades that haunt the fed for years. |
| **6-10** | Functional. Can close fair deals. Occasionally gets outmaneuvered. |
| **11-15** | Sharp. Consistently gets favorable terms. Knows when to push, when to fold. |
| **16-20** | Master dealmaker. Steals talent, wins every trade, locks workers into team-friendly deals. Other feds fear negotiating with them. |

---

## What It Affects ‚úÖ LOCKED

- **Contract Terms:** Salary, length, creative control clauses, no-compete windows
- **Trade Evaluation:** Ability to assess true value vs perceived value
- **Poaching Success:** Likelihood of luring talent from competitors
- **Deal Leverage:** Using information, timing, and relationships to gain advantage
- **Resistance Defusion:** High negotiation can sometimes talk down a resistant worker

## Negotiation in Different Contexts

| Context | Application |
|---------|-------------|
| **Contract Signing** | Determines who gets the better end of the deal |
| **Draft Trades** | Evaluates pick/worker swaps accurately |
| **Mid-Season Trades** | Pulls off steals or gets burned |
| **Talent Poaching** | Convinces workers to jump ship |
| **Worker Resistance** | Can negotiate compromises instead of escalation |
| **Sponsor Deals** | Affects financial terms (with Hype stat) |

---

## Interaction with Other Factors

| Factor | Interaction |
|--------|-------------|
| **Desperation** | Even high negotiators make bad deals when desperate |
| **Leverage** | Having something the other side wants amplifies negotiation |
| **Relationships** | Past history (good or bad) modifies outcomes |
| **Ego (opponent's)** | High-ego opponents may reject fair deals out of pride |
| **Information** | Knowing hidden stats/situations gives advantage |

## Workers with Negotiation Stat

Not all workers have meaningful Negotiation scores. Those who do:
- Veterans with backstage pull
- Workers with agent/producer roles
- Booking Politicians (tag)
- Those with creative control clauses
- Union organizers or locker room leaders

Workers without backstage influence default to low/no negotiation impact.
