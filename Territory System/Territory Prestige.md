## Overview

Territory Prestige represents a territory's reputation, drawing power, and industry standing. High prestige territories attract top talent, secure better TV deals, and produce nationally recognized stars. Low prestige territories struggle to retain talent and may collapse.

---

## Prestige Scale

| Level | Range | Descriptor | Characteristics |
|-------|-------|------------|-----------------|
| **Elite** | 90-100 | "Major League" | National TV, top talent, industry-defining |
| **High** | 70-89 | "Strong Territory" | Regional TV, draws talent, respected |
| **Moderate** | 50-69 | "Solid Territory" | Local TV, adequate talent, stable |
| **Low** | 30-49 | "Struggling Territory" | Limited TV, talent drain, vulnerable |
| **Dying** | 10-29 | "Dying Territory" | No TV, mass exodus, collapse imminent |
| **Dead** | 0-9 | "Defunct" | Closed or absorbed |

## Prestige Tiers

### Tier 1: Major Territories

**Prestige Range:** 80-100
**Characteristics:**
- National or broad regional TV
- Top-tier talent attraction
- Industry influence
- Title holders recognized nationally

**Historical Examples:**
- WWF (1980s national expansion)
- NWA Championship territory
- AJPW (Japan major)

### Tier 2: Strong Territories

**Prestige Range:** 60-79
**Characteristics:**
- Regional TV coverage
- Can develop stars
- Stable economics
- Respected by peers

**Historical Examples:**
- Memphis (mid-80s)
- Mid-South
- World Class (Dallas)

### Tier 3: Minor Territories

**Prestige Range:** 40-59
**Characteristics:**
- Limited TV or none
- Talent pool challenges
- Local audience only
- Dependent on major territory relationships

**Historical Examples:**
- Smaller NWA affiliates
- Independent territories

### Tier 4: Dying/Outlaw

**Prestige Range:** 0-39
**Characteristics:**
- No TV
- Major talent drain
- Economic crisis
- Collapse imminent or operating underground

**Historical Examples:**
- AWA (late 80s decline)
- Regional indies post-territory collapse

### Prestige Collapse

Territories can collapse rapidly when:
1. **Star exodus** (3+ top workers leave)
2. **TV deal lost**
3. **Financial crisis** (can't make payroll)
4. **Hostile acquisition** (bought and stripped)
5. **Scandal** (major public relations disaster)

### Prestige Recovery

Recovering from prestige loss requires:
1. **New investment** (ownership change with capital)
2. **Star acquisition** (major signing)
3. **TV renewal** (new deal)
4. **Creative renaissance** (fresh booking)
5. **Time** (3-5 years minimum for full recovery)
