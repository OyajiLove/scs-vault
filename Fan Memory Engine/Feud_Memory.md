## FEUD TYPES & DURATIONS

| Feud Type | Optimal Duration | Intensity | Payoff Event | Notes |
|-----------|------------------|-----------|--------------|-------|
| **Blood Feud** | 6-12 months | Maximum | Career-defining match (cage, retirement, loser leaves) | Personal hatred, violence escalation |
| **Title Chase** | 3-6 months | High | Championship win/loss | Must culminate in title change or definitive end |

## TRACKING METRICS

### Match History

- Total matches between workers
- Win/loss record
- Match quality average
- Stipulation matches (hardcore, cage, etc.)
- Title matches

### Emotional Peaks

- Betrayals (turn moments)
- Injuries (real or angle)
- Title changes
- Screwjobs/controversies
- Blood/violence escalation
- Personal attacks (family, character)

### Crowd Investment Score (0-100)

- Based on: crowd reactions, segment heat, merchandise sales, social media buzz
- **High Investment (80-100):** Crowd deeply cares, can't miss TV
- **Medium Investment (50-79):** Crowd interested, solid reactions
- **Low Investment (20-49):** Crowd lukewarm, filler feud
- **Dead (0-19):** Crowd doesn't care, change direction

### Feud Fatigue Meter (0-100)

- **Low Fatigue (0-30):** Feud feels fresh, can continue
- **Medium Fatigue (31-60):** Need progression or cool-down
- **High Fatigue (61-85):** Must blow off soon or crowd turns
- **Critical Fatigue (86-100):** Crowd actively rejecting feud, emergency pivot needed

## FEUD PROGRESSION BEATS

### Typical Blood Feud Arc (6-12 months):

**Month 1-2: Setup**

- Inciting incident (betrayal, attack, disrespect)
- First confrontation (promo, pull-apart brawl)
- Initial match (heel wins dirty or interference)

**Month 3-4: Escalation**

- Rematch (babyface wins or screwed again)
- Personal attacks escalate (family, character)
- Stipulation announced (cage, no DQ, etc.)

**Month 5-6: Peak**

- Blow-off match at major show
- Definitive winner
- Feud ends (or... swerve betrayal starts new arc)

**Variation: Extended Blood Feud (7-12 months)**

- Add: Injury angle (kayfabe or shoot)
- Add: Third-party interference (manager, faction)
- Add: False finish (screwjob extends feud)
- Peak: Ultra-violent blow-off (Hell in Cell, exploding ring, etc.)

## FEUD FATIGUE MECHANICS

### Fatigue Builds When:

- Same match repeated without variation (3+ times)
- No storyline progression between matches
- Predictable outcomes (babyface always screwed)
- Overexposure (every show, multiple segments)
- Lack of escalation (no stakes increase)

### Fatigue Reduces When:

- Time apart (cool-down period)
- Fresh stipulation added
- Third party adds new dynamic
- Character development/turn
- Storyline twist (betrayal, alliance, reveal)

### Critical Threshold:

**If Fatigue ‚â• 80 and Investment < 50:**

- Crowd actively wants feud to end
- Segments get "boring" chants
- Match ratings suffer despite quality
- **Action Required:** Blow off immediately or pivot completely

## CALLBACK SYSTEM

### Dormant Feud Reactivation

**When Feud Has Been Dormant 1+ Years:**

- Memory decay has occurred
- Nostalgia factor builds
- **Callback Pop:** Returns can generate huge reaction

**Example Progression:**

1. **Active Feud (2023):** Investment 90, Fatigue 75 ‚Üí Blow off at PPV
2. **Dormancy (2024-2026):** Memory decays to 60, Fatigue resets to 0
3. **Surprise Return (2027):** Nostalgia pop +30, Investment spikes to 85
4. **Quick Program (1-2 months):** One more match, then done for good

### Callback Opportunities:

- Anniversary shows (WrestleMania rematch 10 years later)
- Retirement tours (one last match with old rival)
- Hall of Fame inductions (reconciliation angle)
- Surprise returns (attacking old enemy)
- Tag team reunion (former rivals team up)

## MULTI-PERSON FEUDS

### Three-Way Feuds

- Tracks relationships between all three workers
- Can have: A hates B, B hates C, C neutral to A
- Payoff: Triple Threat or elimination-style match
- **Risk:** Confusing if not clearly defined

### Faction Wars

- Tracks: Faction A vs. Faction B
- Individual match-ups within larger war
- Can have: Betrayals, defections, leadership changes
- Payoff: War Games, Survivor Series, Faction disbands
- **Risk:** Too many people dilutes individual stories

## MEMORY ANCHORS FROM FEUDS

### High-Memory Feud Moments:

- **Betrayal Turn:** Austin/McMahon, Mega Powers explode
- **Career-Ending Match:** HBK/Flair, Undertaker/HBK
- **Blood Bath:** Cactus/Triple H, Bret/Austin
- **Title Change:** Hogan/Andre, Bryan/Cena
- **Screwjob:** Montreal, Fingerpoke of Doom

These become **Anchors** (memory markers) that:

- Generate commentary callbacks forever
- Affect future storylines (grudge never forgotten)
- Elevate worker legacies
- Can be referenced in other feuds

## CALLBACK EFFECTIVENESS DECAY (#185)

üìå **Source:** Vol 1 Extraction #185

### References to Past Feuds/Moments Lose Power Over Time

| Time Since Event | Callback Power |
|------------------|----------------|
| **1-3 months** | Full emotional impact |
| **6-12 months** | Moderate impact, hardcore fans respond |
| **1-3 years** | Nostalgic pop, new fans confused |
| **5+ years** | Only works for legendary moments |

**Exception:** Truly legendary moments (Hogan/Andre, Austin 3:16, Montreal Screwjob) retain power indefinitely.

## FEUD HEAT DECAY (#201)

üìå **Source:** Vol 1 Extraction #201

### How Feud Intensity Changes Over Time

| Status | Heat Level |
|--------|------------|
| **Active (matches scheduled)** | Peak heat maintained |
| **Dormant (no interaction)** | Decays 10% per month |
| **Referenced (promos only)** | Decays 5% per month |
| **Revived (new incident)** | Can restore to 80% of peak |

**Application:** Long-running feuds need periodic fuel to stay hot. Scheduled matches, promos, or incidents prevent decay.
