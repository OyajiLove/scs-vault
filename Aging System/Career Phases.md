## Overview

Every worker progresses through distinct career phases, each with unique characteristics for skill development, booking expectations, and physical performance. Phase transitions are driven by age, performance, and accumulated wear.

---

## Career Phase Definitions

### Young Phase (Debut to 27)

| Aspect | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Duration** | Debut age to approximately 27 |
| **Skill Growth** | +1 to +3 per year in trained skills |
| **Physical Peak** | Not yet reached |
| **Booking Role** | Rising talent, builds credibility |
| **Key Tags** | "Green", "Rising Star", "Future Ace" |
| **Mental Growth** | Psychology and Composure still developing |

**Characteristics:**
- Highest learning potential
- Mentor relationships most effective
- Can survive risky styles longer
- Building reputation and fan connection
- Mistakes are more forgivable

### Peak Phase (28-35)

| Aspect | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Duration** | Approximately 28-35 (varies by style) |
| **Skill Growth** | +0 to +1 per year (maintenance) |
| **Physical Peak** | Optimal performance |
| **Booking Role** | Main event caliber, carrying promotions |
| **Key Tags** | "Prime", "Main Event Star", "In Demand" |
| **Mental Growth** | Psychology/Composure can still increase |

**Characteristics:**
- Best balance of skill and physicality
- Highest drawing potential
- Most valuable contract years
- Title reign opportunities
- Legacy-building matches
- Style may need minor adjustments late peak

### Veteran Phase (36-42)

| Aspect | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Duration** | Approximately 36-42 |
| **Skill Decline** | -1 per year in physical stats |
| **Physical State** | Declining, accumulated wear showing |
| **Booking Role** | Upper card, putting over younger talent |
| **Key Tags** | "Veteran Presence", "Ring General", "Territory Legend" |
| **Mental Growth** | Can still improve psychology, adaptability |

**Characteristics:**
- Experience compensates for physical decline
- Match psychology often peaks here
- Must adapt style (less high-flying, more psychology)
- Mentoring opportunities
- Ego management crucial (accepting reduced role)
- Injury risk increases
- Part-time schedules may be needed

### Twilight Phase (43+)

| Aspect | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Duration** | 43 until retirement |
| **Skill Decline** | -1 to -2 per year in physical stats |
| **Physical State** | Significant limitations |
| **Booking Role** | Special attraction, legends matches |
| **Key Tags** | "Legend", "Part-Timer", "Living History" |
| **Mental State** | Experience maximized, body failing |

**Characteristics:**
- Very limited in-ring schedule
- Matches must be protected
- Nostalgia pop from appearances
- Risk of embarrassing performances
- Retirement consideration active
- Legacy protection important
- May transition to non-wrestling role

## Phase-Based Booking Expectations

| Phase | Expected Card Position | Match Length | Schedule |

## Style-Specific Phase Adjustments

Different wrestling styles have different career trajectories:

| Style | Peak Start | Peak End | Notes |
|-------|------------|----------|-------|
| **High Flyer** | 24 | 32 | Early peak, body breaks down faster |
| **Technical** | 26 | 38 | Longer peak, skill-based |
| **Power** | 27 | 36 | Strength holds, joints suffer |
| **Striker** | 26 | 35 | Moderate peak window |
| **Submission** | 28 | 40 | Longest peak, technique over athletics |
| **Psychology** | 30 | 45 | Peaks late, lasts longest |
| **Deathmatch** | 25 | 33 | Short peak, body accumulates damage |
