## Overview

Career Arc Templates define the narrative shape of a worker's career. These patterns emerge from the interaction of talent, booking, personality, and circumstance. Arcs help generate realistic career narratives and provide booking guidance for AI bookers.

---

## Core Arc Templates

### Meteoric Rise

**Pattern:** Rapid ascent from debut to main event, sustained success
**Duration:** 2-5 years to main event, 10-15 year peak

| Phase | Characteristics |
|-------|-----------------|
| **Young** | Immediately impressive, pushed fast |
| **Peak** | Dominant champion, face of promotion |
| **Veteran** | Transitions gracefully, mentor role |
| **Twilight** | Legend status, special appearances |

**Requirements:**
- High natural talent (multiple stats 16+)
- Strong charisma or unique look
- Booking support from management
- No major scandals or injuries

**Examples:** The Rock, John Cena, Rikidozan, Hulk Hogan (pre-nWo)

**Risks:**
- Early burnout if pushed too fast
- Crowd rejection if push outpaces organic connection
- Ego inflation, backstage politics

---

### Journeyman Grind

**Pattern:** Slow, steady career without breakthrough moment
**Duration:** 15-25 years of consistent work

| Phase | Characteristics |
|-------|-----------------|
| **Young** | Shows promise, never gets big break |
| **Peak** | Reliable midcard, occasional upper card |
| **Veteran** | Respected worker, enhancement role |
| **Twilight** | Quiet fade, may train or manage |

**Requirements:**
- Solid but not exceptional talent
- Professional attitude (Loyal Soldier archetype)
- Bad timing or booking neglect
- Consistent work rate

**Examples:** Dean Malenko, Tito Santana, Chris Masters, Shelton Benjamin

**Characteristics:**
- Never holds world title (or brief transitional)
- Praised by peers, overlooked by promoters
- May achieve late-career recognition
- Often become excellent trainers/agents

---

### Tragic Fall

**Pattern:** High peak followed by dramatic decline
**Duration:** 5-10 year peak, rapid 3-5 year decline

| Phase | Characteristics |
|-------|-----------------|
| **Young** | Promising debut, early success |
| **Peak** | Main event status, world champion |
| **Decline Trigger** | Injury, scandal, addiction, burnout |
| **Fall** | Rapid descent, embarrassing late matches |
| **End** | Premature retirement or death |

**Requirements:**
- Self-Destructive archetype common
- Major injury or scandal
- Substance abuse issues
- Poor support system

**Examples:** Ultimate Warrior (career), Jake Roberts (pre-recovery), Dynamite Kid, Chris Benoit

**Warning Signs:**
- Increased injury frequency
- Erratic behavior
- Public incidents
- Declining match quality despite booking support

---

### Late-Career Redemption

**Pattern:** Struggling early/mid career, breakthrough in 30s-40s
**Duration:** 10-15 years of struggle, 5-10 year peak

| Phase | Characteristics |
|-------|-----------------|
| **Young** | Undervalued, wrong gimmicks, bad booking |
| **Mid-Career** | Journeyman status, frustration |
| **Breakthrough** | Finally gets right opportunity |
| **Late Peak** | Becomes star after years of work |
| **Legacy** | Beloved for perseverance |

**Requirements:**
- Genuine talent that was misused
- Patience and resilience (Never Say Die)
- Change of scenery or creative control
- Crowd connection despite booking

**Examples:** DDP, Batista, Goldust (reinventions), JBL, Jinder Mahal

**Triggers for Breakthrough:**
- Promotion change
- Gimmick overhaul
- Veteran partnership
- Injury to pushed talent creates opening
- Crowd rebellion forces push

---

### Flash in the Pan

**Pattern:** Brief, intense success followed by complete fade
**Duration:** 1-3 year peak, 5-10 year decline

| Phase | Characteristics |
|-------|-----------------|
| **Rise** | Sudden explosion of popularity |
| **Peak** | White-hot for brief period |
| **Fade** | Rapid decline, can't recapture magic |
| **End** | Forgotten or ironically remembered |

**Requirements:**
- One-dimensional appeal (look, gimmick, moment)
- Limited in-ring ability or adaptability
- Overexposure burns crowd out
- No character evolution

**Examples:** Goldberg (late WCW), Ahmed Johnson, Ultimate Warrior, Ryback

**Warning Signs:**
- Crowd excitement fading despite push
- No successful character evolution
- Reliance on same spots/promos
- Injury exposing limitations

---

### Political Survivor

**Pattern:** Career defined by backstage navigation, not in-ring work
**Duration:** 15-20+ years through multiple regimes

| Phase | Characteristics |
|-------|-----------------|
| **Young** | Identifies power structures early |
| **Peak** | Aligned with decision-makers |
| **Challenges** | Survives regime changes |
| **Veteran** | Becomes power broker |
| **Legacy** | Controversial, respected for survival |

**Requirements:**
- Backstage Politician archetype
- High Ego, moderate-high Paranoia
- Willingness to politic
- Adaptability to new bosses

**Examples:** Triple H, Hulk Hogan, Kevin Nash, Ric Flair (latter career)

**Characteristics:**
- Long title reigns despite criticism
- Survives controversies that end others
- Develops reputation (positive or negative)
- May become booker/executive

---

### Cult Legend

**Pattern:** Never mainstream success, worshipped by niche audience
**Duration:** Full career outside mainstream

| Phase | Characteristics |
|-------|-----------------|
| **Young** | Develops unique style/persona |
| **Career** | Works independent or niche circuits |
| **Recognition** | Gains cult following |
| **Legacy** | Influences future generations |

**Requirements:**
- Unique style or character
- Unwillingness or inability to compromise for mainstream
- Underground scene support
- Critical acclaim without commercial success

**Examples:** Mick Foley (pre-WWF), Eddie Kingston (pre-AEW), Terry Funk (Japan), Bruiser Brody

**Characteristics:**
- Hardcore fan devotion
- May achieve late mainstream recognition
- Influences many imitators
- Often becomes training/booking influence

## Arc Modification

Arcs can be modified by:

| Factor | Effect on Arc |
|--------|---------------|
| **Major Injury** | Can truncate any arc, force early decline |
| **Scandal** | Can trigger tragic fall or derail meteoric rise |
| **Booking Change** | New regime may reposition worker |
| **Turn** | Successful heel/face turn can restart arc |
| **Promotion Jump** | Fresh start can create late redemption |

- Tragic Fall: 10% of workers
